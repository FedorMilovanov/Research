# V80 — Spiritual Pride, False Humility, Self-Love, Man-Pleasing, and Religious Self-Display

**Purpose:** add a Puritan/Reformed diagnostic layer on **spiritual pride**, **false humility**, **self-love**, **man-pleasing**, **party-spirit**, **religious self-display**, and the subtle way a person can turn even repentance, experiences, strictness, zeal, gifts, and self-examination into a new righteousness.

This layer is needed because the heart-series has now gathered many diagnostic tools. But the heart can corrupt even diagnosis itself:

```text
A man can be proud of orthodoxy.
A man can be proud of humility.
A man can be proud of repentance.
A man can be proud of being anti-legalistic.
A man can be proud of “seeing idols everywhere.”
```

Article-ready thesis:

> Самая тонкая форма идолопоклонства сердца — когда человек делает идол не из грубого удовольствия, а из своего духовного образа: “я вижу глубже”, “я каюсь правильнее”, “я строже”, “я свободнее”, “я не такой, как эти плотские люди”. Пуритане били не только по миру в сердце, но и по религиозному “я”.

---

## 1. Why this layer must come after duties, conscience, and temptation

V77 traced temptation from first motions to consent. V78 protected conscience from tyranny and scruples. V79 protected duties from both legal reliance and antinomian neglect.

V80 now asks a deeper question:

```text
What happens when the heart makes a throne out of its spirituality?
```

Spiritual pride is dangerous because it can hide under the most pious language.

Diagnostic map:

```text
Gross pride: “I am great.”
Moral pride: “I am better than the immoral.”
Doctrinal pride: “I see what others do not.”
Experiential pride: “My experiences prove my height.”
Humility-pride: “My abasement is impressive.”
Anti-legalistic pride: “I am freer and more gospel-centered than legalists.”
Diagnostic pride: “I discern hearts better than others.”
```

Pastoral warning:

> The more advanced the diagnostic work becomes, the more urgently it must be guarded from pride. Otherwise the medicine becomes poison.

## 2. Richard Baxter — pride is commonly condemned while secretly cherished

**Primary source:** Richard Baxter, *A Christian Directory*, Part I, “Directions against Pride, and for Humility.”

Source anchors:

- EEBO/TCP: https://quod.lib.umich.edu/e/eebo/A26892.0001.001/1%3A5.6.5?rgn=div3%3Bview%3Dfulltext
- Digital Puritan individual PDF: https://www.digitalpuritan.net/Digital%20Puritan%20Resources/Baxter%2C%20Richard/A%20Body%20of%20Divinity%20%28vol.2%29%20Individual%20Files/Against%20Pride.pdf
- Monergism Part I PDF: https://www.monergism.com/thethreshold/sdg/baxter/A%20Christian%20Directory%2C%20Part%20I_%20-%20Richard%20Baxter.pdf

Baxter’s first contribution is surgical: pride is infamous in name but undiscerned in nature. Men speak against it while cherishing it. Therefore the first cure is not a slogan against pride, but discovery of what pride actually is.

Core distinction:

```text
Pride is not merely confidence, zeal, seriousness, rank, knowledge, or assurance.
Pride is inordinate self-exaltation: desiring to appear greater, wiser, or better than one is.
Humility is not pretending gifts do not exist.
Humility is right self-estimation under God and contentedness with one’s assigned place.
```

This is gold because Baxter guards against both false accusations and real pride:

- A person may be wrongly called proud for refusing sin with the crowd.
- A preacher may be wrongly called proud for zealously teaching truth.
- A believer may be wrongly called proud for having assurance and thankfulness.
- But a person may also be deeply proud in religious duties, knowledge, reputation, and party identity.

Article-ready sentence:

> Baxter разрушает грубую схему: не всякая твёрдость — гордость, не всякая уверенность — гордость, не всякая ревность — гордость. Но он одновременно показывает, что настоящая гордость может жить прямо в религиозных обязанностях, в желании выглядеть мудрее, святее и выше своего места.

---

## 3. Baxter — false humility can make a proud man seem humble

In the same section Baxter explicitly warns about **counterfeits of humility**. This is a needed diagnostic category because false humility is not the opposite of pride; it may be pride wearing low clothing.

Core distinction:

```text
True humility: low before God, truthful about gifts, willing to serve in assigned place.
False humility: performs smallness in order to be seen, affirmed, or spiritually distinguished.
```

Pastoral use:

```text
Do not call every gifted person proud.
Do not call every self-deprecating person humble.
Watch what the heart seeks: God’s glory, neighbour’s good, truth, service — or self-display in a humble costume.
```

Article-ready sentence:

> Ложное смирение — это не слишком большое смирение. Это гордость, которая поняла, что в религиозной среде выгодно выглядеть маленькой.

## 4. Jonathan Edwards — spiritual pride can masquerade as gospel boldness, anti-legalism, and humility

**Primary source:** Jonathan Edwards, *Religious Affections*.

Source anchors:

- CCEL, true Christian spirit / spiritual pride and zeal: https://www.ccel.org/ccel/edwards/affections.vi.vii.html
- CCEL, legal spirit and spiritual pride: https://www.ccel.org/ccel/edwards/affections.vi.v.html
- Monergism PDF: https://www.monergism.com/thethreshold/sdg/edwards/A%20Treatise%20on%20Religious%20Affecti%20-%20Jonathan%20Edwards.pdf

Edwards is not an English Puritan in the strict chronological sense, but he is a first-rank Reformed heir of Puritan experimental divinity and extremely valuable on this topic.

His gold:

```text
Pretended boldness for Christ may arise from pride.
Spiritual pride seeks distinction, singularity, and party elevation.
True zeal is first against evil in one's own heart.
True Christian zeal is the heat of love, not bitterness against persons.
A legal spirit can hide under anti-legal slogans.
A man may be proud of experiences, humiliation, faith, discoveries, or being “evangelical.”
```

Especially useful:

> A person may cry down legalism and still be full of legal pride, because he is lifted up by his experiences, gospel language, discoveries, humility, or anti-legal identity.

Article-ready sentence:

> Edwards даёт страшно современный предохранитель: можно громко разоблачать законничество и в тот же момент жить законнической гордостью — только опираться уже не на грубые дела, а на “евангельские” переживания, глубину, опыт, свободу и красивое смирение.

## 5. Edwards — zeal is not bitterness; boldness is not aggression

Edwards distinguishes Christian fortitude and zeal from fierce, bitter, party-making religion.

Diagnostic grid:

```text
False boldness: enjoys provoking opponents; seeks distinction; fights “carnal” people to be exalted among its own party.
True boldness: will be despised by friends and foes rather than offend Christ.
False zeal: chiefly attacks the sins of others.
True zeal: chiefly opposes the enemies of God in one’s own heart.
False zeal: heat without love.
True zeal: heat of holy love.
```

Article-ready sentence:

> Настоящая ревность сначала воюет не с “теми людьми”, а с врагами Бога в собственном сердце. Если ревность делает человека жёстким, ядовитым, партийным и сладко-воинственным, это может быть не огонь Духа, а жар духовной гордости.

## 6. Thomas Brooks — humility grows around the unsearchable riches of Christ

**Primary source:** Thomas Brooks, *The Unsearchable Riches of Christ*.

Source anchors:

- GraceGems text: https://www.gracegems.org/Brooks/unsearchable_riches_of_christ.htm
- Monergism page: https://www.monergism.com/unsearchable-riches-christ-ebook
- Digital Puritan Works vol.3: https://www.digitalpuritan.net/Digital%20Puritan%20Resources/Brooks%2C%20Thomas/The%20Complete%20Works%20of%20Thomas%20Brooks%20%28vol.3%29.pdf

Brooks structures a large section around Ephesians 3:8: “less than the least of all saints” and the “unsearchable riches of Christ.” The very table of contents is valuable:

```text
Those who are lowest in their own esteem are highest in God's esteem.
Eighteen properties of a humble soul.
Nine directions and helps to keep us humble and low.
The dangerous nature of pride in nine propositions.
Six ways wherein pride shows itself.
All saints are not of equal size and growth in grace.
Supports and comforts for weak Christians.
Duties of strong saints to the weak.
```

This is gold because Brooks refuses both spiritual pride and contempt for weak saints.

Article-ready sentence:

> Brooks ставит смирение не рядом с презрением к себе как психологией, а рядом с “неисследимым богатством Христа”. Чем богаче Христос для души, тем меньше душа нуждается в самовозвышении; и тем осторожнее она относится к слабым, потому что не все святые одного роста.

## 7. Thomas Watson — a humble man complains more of his heart than his condition

**Primary source:** Thomas Watson, *The Godly Man’s Picture*.

Source anchors:

- Digital Puritan PDF: https://www.digitalpuritan.net/Digital%20Puritan%20Resources/Watson%2C%20Thomas/The%20Godly%20Man%27s%20Picture%20%5BOTW%5D.pdf
- GraceGems section: https://www.gracegems.org/Watson/godly_mans_picture10.htm
- FiveSolas excerpt: https://www.fivesolas.com/Watson/godlyhmbl.htm
- IA 1758 edition locator: https://archive.org/details/bim_eighteenth-century_the-godlymans-picture-_watson-thomas_1758

Watson is useful as a plain pastoral voice. His humility section shows that a godly man is not merely externally modest; his complaint is directed at his own heart. This fits the current project because the heart-series must not turn into blaming circumstances, other people, culture, or demons while sparing the self.

Core distinction:

```text
Proud sorrow: “Why is my condition so hard?”
Humble sorrow: “Why is my heart so unbelieving, cold, proud, and self-protecting?”
```

Article-ready sentence:

> Watson помогает отличить смирение от театральной печали: смиренный человек не просто жалуется на тяжёлые обстоятельства; он видит, что самая опасная проблема рядом — его собственное сердце.

## 8. John Flavel — prosperity and success reveal whether grace checks pride

**Primary source:** John Flavel, *The Touchstone of Sincerity*.

Source anchors:

- Digital Puritan PDF: https://www.digitalpuritan.net/Digital%20Puritan%20Resources/Flavel%2C%20John/Works%20%28Vol.5%29%20Ind%20Titles/%5BJF%5D%20The%20Touchstone%20of%20Sincerity.pdf
- IA locator: https://archive.org/details/touchstoneofsinc00flav

Flavel gives a very practical test: prosperity, success, honour, and enlargement draw out dross. But in the upright heart, grace gives a check to pride. This is not perfectionism: he admits that believers may have much dross discovered under prosperity. The test is whether pride lodges quietly or is checked, abased, and carried back to God.

Core distinction:

```text
Graceless prosperity: success feeds self.
Gracious prosperity: success reveals remaining dross, but grace checks pride and raises God high.
```

Article-ready sentence:

> Flavel не говорит: “если при успехе в тебе поднялась гордость, ты не христианин”. Он говорит тоньше: успех раскрывает дросс; вопрос в том, позволяет ли сердце гордости спокойно поселиться, или благодать даёт ей отпор и возвращает славу Богу.

## 10. Pastoral diagnostic grid — pride vs lawful confidence, false humility vs true humility

### A. Not necessarily pride

```text
Knowing what God has taught you.
Being thankful for grace.
Having assurance in Christ.
Refusing to sin with the crowd.
Teaching truth zealously.
Correcting error with charity.
Using gifts for the church.
Maintaining rightful office/order.
Rejoicing in God’s work in you.
```

### B. Dangerous signs of pride

```text
Wanting to appear wiser, holier, deeper, stricter, freer, or more discerning than you are.
Needing others to notice your humility, repentance, sacrifice, or depth.
Being more offended when your reputation is touched than when God is dishonoured.
Loving the role of corrector more than the good of the corrected.
Calling harshness “zeal.”
Calling party-bitterness “boldness for Christ.”
Despising weak saints because you see more than they do.
Turning experiences, theology, repentance, or diagnostics into righteousness.
```

### C. False humility

```text
Self-deprecation that secretly asks to be contradicted.
Public abasement that seeks admiration.
Refusing lawful service under cover of humility.
Calling God-given gifts “nothing” instead of using them faithfully.
Appearing low in words while being unteachable in spirit.
```

### D. True humility

```text
Truthful about gifts, but not self-exalting.
Low before God, but not cowardly before men.
Willing to be hidden if Christ is honoured.
Willing to be corrected.
More suspicious of one’s own pride than impressed by one’s humility.
More severe toward sin in oneself than toward weakness in others.
```

  Pride belongs to fallen nature; grace gives a new principle of self-abasement; glory removes pride entirely.

False peace:
  A man may be peaceful because he is proud of his religion.

  Temporary faith can be joyful and zealous but not humble before Christ.

  Weak believers may have pride, but they grieve and fight it.

  The most subtle idol may be the religious self.

## 12. Article-ready paragraph

> Есть гордость грубая: “я велик”. Есть гордость моральная: “я лучше этих грешников”. Есть гордость богословская: “я вижу то, чего не видят они”. Есть гордость евангельская: “я не законник, как они”. Есть гордость покаянная: “моё сокрушение глубже”. Есть гордость смиренная: “моё смирение заметно”. Поэтому зрелая диагностика сердца должна быть обращена не только против денег, комфорта, похоти и статуса, но и против религиозного “я”, которое умеет сделать престол даже из борьбы с идолами.

## 13. Compact formulas for the series

```text
Not every firmness is pride.
Not every self-deprecation is humility.
Not every boldness is courage.
Not every zeal is love.
Not every anti-legalism is gospel freedom.
Not every spiritual experience is spiritual poverty.

True humility sees gifts without enthroning self.
False humility lowers the voice while raising the self.
Spiritual pride can wear doctrinal accuracy, zeal, tears, liberty, discipline, and even repentance.
```

Most useful one-liner:

> Самый скрытый идол сердца — не всегда удовольствие; иногда это образ себя как духовного человека.
