# V66 ADDENDUM — Sibbes / Goodwin / Flavel: pastoral states without forcing a fourfold scheme

**Important rule:** do not force every Puritan into Boston's exact fourfold structure. Use each author for his own gold.

## 1. John Flavel — transition from nature to grace

Primary locator:

- CCEL, John Flavel, `Method of Grace in the Gospel Redemption`: https://ccel.org/ccel/flavel/grace/grace.xx.html
- Digital Puritan full text of Flavel vol. 2: https://www.digitalpuritan.net/Digital%20Puritan%20Resources/Flavel%2C%20John/The%20Whole%20Works%20of%20John%20Flavel%20%28vol.2%29.txt.html

Function:

```text
state of nature → state of grace
```

Flavel is useful to keep the transition Christ-centered: the believer is not self-improved out of nature; he is translated into grace through Christ.

## 2. Thomas Goodwin — state of glory as a pastoral horizon

Primary locator:

- Thomas Goodwin, `Of the Blessed State of Glory Which the Saints Possess After Death`: https://www.monergism.com/thethreshold/sdg/goodwin/Of%20the%20Blessed%20State%20of%20Glory%20W%20-%20Thomas%20Goodwin.pdf
- Digital Puritan text of Goodwin works vol. 7: https://www.digitalpuritan.net/Digital%20Puritan%20Resources/Goodwin%2C%20Thomas/The%20Works%20of%20Thomas%20Goodwin%20%28vol.7%29.txt.html

Function:

```text
state of nature = glory warns and calls
state of grace = glory loosens the heart from the world
state of glory = heart's final holy blessedness
```

Goodwin should be used near the end of the article, not as the main map.

## 3. Richard Sibbes — corruption wholly in nature, in part in the best

Primary / near-primary locator:

- Sibbes, `The Church's Complaint and Confidence`: https://www.apuritansmind.com/puritan-favorites/puritan-favorites-richard-sibbes-1577-1635/the-churchs-complaint-and-confidence-a-sermon-by-richard-sibbes/

Function:

```text
wicked / state of nature = corruption wholly
best / renewed = corruption in part
```

This is important for tone: do not say that the believer's remaining corruption means “nothing changed”; also do not say that corruption is harmless.

## 4. Brooks / Watson remain secondary helpers here

Use Brooks/Watson only if needed for assurance, catechetical clarity, or pastoral tenderness. Do not over-cite them in the four-state section.

```text
true grace may exist without assurance

```

Use Brooks whenever the article risks crushing weak believers by measuring regeneration through emotional brightness or confident assurance.

## Walter Marshall — sanctification from union with Christ

Primary locators:

- https://www.monergism.com/thethreshold/articles/onsite/GospelMystery.pdf
- https://www.digitalpuritan.net/Digital%20Puritan%20Resources/Marshall%2C%20Walter/The%20Gospel%20Mystery%20of%20Sanctification%20Opened.pdf

faith receives Christ with all endowments necessary for holy practice
union with Christ precedes gospel holiness

Use Marshall to keep mortification / discipline / habits from turning into a sanctified self-help system.

# V71 insertion — Sibbes / Goodwin / Bridge / Gurnall for darkness and wounded believers

## Richard Sibbes — The Soul’s Conflict With Itself

Use Sibbes as the bridge between exposing false peace and healing wounded spirits. His table of contents alone marks key pastoral categories: discouragements from without/within, excessive dejection, difference between good men and others in conflicts with sin, true comforters, promises, evidences, and quieting the spirit in troubles for sin.

Article-use thesis: self-examination must not become self-destruction. A soul can be sincerely troubled, excessively dejected, and still be a living soul needing ordered comfort.

## Thomas Goodwin — A Child of Light Walking in Darkness

Use Goodwin to distinguish:

```text
child of light walking in darkness
child of darkness walking in self-kindled light
```

Goodwin is especially valuable because he refuses emotional simplifications: the true believer may lack felt light, while the unregenerate may have natural/legal comfort and outward religious brightness.

## William Bridge — A Lifting Up for the Downcast

Use Bridge as a cases-of-conscience map for discouraged Christians: great sins, weak grace, failed duties, want of assurance, temptation, desertion, affliction, unserviceableness. This prevents one-size-fits-all diagnosis.

## William Gurnall — affrighting temptations

Use Gurnall for intrusive/terrifying temptations: atheism, blasphemy, despair. Critical distinction: a thought may be present and terrifying without being loved or consented to by the will.

## V72 insertion — Brooks / Sibbes / Watson / Flavel bank expanded for backsliding and repentance

### Richard Sibbes — The Returning Backslider

Primary-source anchor: *The Returning Backslider*, Hosea 14 sermons, 1639 EEBO-TCP record.

Use: restores the category of a backslider who must return, not an instantly discarded soul. Mercy is free and large, but the return includes humiliation and repentance.

Source: https://quod.lib.umich.edu/e/eebo2/A12190.0001.001?view=toc

### Richard Sibbes — The Danger of Backsliding

Use: backsliding can begin when the heart hangs loose from God and joins itself to the creature as prop/comfort/security. This is crucial for the “idols” theme without overcalling every lawful comfort an idol.

Source: https://www.digitalpuritan.net/Digital%20Puritan%20Resources/Sibbes%2C%20Richard/The%20Complete%20Works%20of%20Richard%20Sibbes%20%28vol.7%29%20Ind%20Works/%5BRS%5D%20The%20Danger%20of%20Backsliding.pdf

### Thomas Brooks — Precious Remedies Against Satan’s Devices

Use: Brooks explicitly writes for those in Christ/out of Christ, growing/decaying, tempted/deserted, with assurance/without assurance. This is a diagnostic map, not a one-size-fits-all rebuke.

Source: https://quod.lib.umich.edu/e/eebo2/A77614.0001.001/1:5.12?rgn=div2;view=fulltext

### Thomas Watson — Doctrine of Repentance

Use: strong distinction between counterfeit repentance and true repentance. Legal terror, resolution, and selective reform are not true repentance.

Source: https://www.gracegems.org/Watson/repentance.htm


## V73 addendum — Flavel and Watson on confirmed grace

**Flavel, Keeping the Heart.** Use as the major source for daily heart-work after regeneration. His key contribution is the order: the heart must first be set right by grace, then kept by diligent use of means.

**Watson, The Godly Man's Picture.** Use as a mark-of-grace source with caution: marks expose hypocrisy and confirm true grace, but they must not become the ground of peace with God. Watson is also realistic about smoke, corruption, distrust, passion, and weak discernibility of grace in true believers.

# V74 INSERT — Watson / Flavel / Goodwin on holy affection and Christ as object

## Thomas Watson — The Saint’s Spiritual Delight

Use Watson as a practical Puritan bridge between Edwards-style affection analysis and everyday piety. Watson says the child of God serves with delight, not merely as a pressed soldier; yet he explicitly allows that a hypocrite may also delight in religion, but carnally and temporarily.

Key use: delight must be tested by object, voluntariness, constancy, and relation to God’s word.

## John Flavel — Christ Altogether Lovely

Use Flavel to keep the article from becoming merely a negative diagnostic machine. Christ’s loveliness is the positive pole: true affection is not merely fear, relief, or religious experience, but a growing sight of Christ’s excellence in person, offices and relations.

## Thomas Goodwin — Heart of Christ in Heaven

Use Goodwin to comfort weak believers after diagnostic exposure. Christ’s exaltation has not made his heart cold toward his members under sin and misery. True diagnosis must lead to the compassionate High Priest, not to endless self-suspicion.
