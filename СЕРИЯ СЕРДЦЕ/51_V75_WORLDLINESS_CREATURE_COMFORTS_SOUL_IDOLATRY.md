# V75 — Worldliness, Creature Comforts, Soul-Idolatry, and Lawful Enjoyment

**Purpose:** deepen the series on the heart by adding a careful Puritan/Reformed layer on lawful comforts, worldly love, creature-dependence, prosperity, fear, trust, pleasure, and inward idolatry.

This file is **not** a general anti-comfort rant. Its purpose is precisely the opposite: to stop the article from calling every lawful affection an idol, while still exposing the point at which a creature becomes a functional refuge, treasure, fear, trust, or ruling love.

Core thesis:

```text
A creature is not an idol because it is loved.
A creature becomes idolatrous when it receives the love, trust, fear, delight, rest, obedience, hope, or dependence due to God.
Lawful comforts are gifts when they lead the heart upward to God and service.
They become snares when they become the heart's chief end, safety, sweetness, or identity.
```

---

## 1. Why this layer is needed

Earlier files already established:

- Boston / 1689 / Vincent: four states — innocence, nature/sin, grace, glory;
- Owen: sin remaining vs sin reigning;
- Burgess / Mead / Shepard / Dyke: counterfeit grace and temporary faith;
- Goodwin / Bridge / Sibbes: true believers can walk in darkness;
- Baxter / Flavel / Scudder: weak grace must be strengthened into daily, established godliness;
- Edwards / Watson / Flavel: affections must be tested by object, root, fruit, humility, holiness.

V75 adds a missing pastoral precision:

1. not every attachment is idolatry;
2. not every desire is sinful;
3. not every comfort is fleshly;
4. not every prosperity is proof of worldliness;
5. but every ruling creature-love is spiritually dangerous.

The article should not say: “If you enjoy comfort, you have an idol.”

The article should say: “Ask what the comfort is doing in the heart: does it serve gratitude, love, obedience, and communion with God, or has it become a substitute for God?”

## 2. Richard Baxter — lawful love of creatures vs sinful love of creatures

**Primary source:** Richard Baxter, *A Christian Directory* (1673), EEBO/TCP, Chapter IV, Part VI, “Directions against Covetousness, or Love of Riches, and against worldly Cares.”

Source anchor:

- EEBO/TCP item: `A26892.0001.001`
- URL: https://quod.lib.umich.edu/e/eebo/A26892.0001.001/1:5.6.6?rgn=div3;view=fulltext

### 2.1. Baxter explicitly rejects the crude formula “all creature-love is sin”

Baxter says that **all love of the creature, the world, or riches is not sin**. He gives several reasons:

- God’s works are good as His works;
- creatures can be loved for God’s sake;
- they are like a glass in which we see the Creator;
- their sweetness is a drop from God’s goodness;
- they can lead the mind up to God;
- they sustain the body for service;
- they can support our journey to heaven;
- wealth can enable relief of the needy and good works.

This is extremely important for the series because it prevents a neurotic/idol-hunting model.

Article-ready formulation:

> Пуританская диагностика не говорит: “если ты любишь дар, ты идолопоклонник”. Baxter прямо различает законную любовь к творению и мирскую любовь. Дар можно любить как Божий дар, как средство служения, благодарности и помощи ближнему. Грех начинается там, где дар перестаёт вести сердце к Даятелю и становится материалом для гордости, плоти, ложной безопасности или высшего счастья.

### 2.2. Baxter’s diagnostic key: end, order, and predominance

Baxter defines sinful worldliness by **end and order**, not simply by possession or enjoyment.

A thing becomes worldly when riches/comforts are loved, desired, or sought:

- more for the flesh than for God;
- more for worldly prosperity than salvation/service;
- to satisfy pride or domination;
- beyond what helps duty, soul, and service.

Baxter’s key diagnostic sentence in substance: the question is not only “how much?” but **to what use, to what end, and in what order?**

Article-ready formulation:

> Главный вопрос не “сколько у тебя есть?” и не “нравится ли тебе это?”, а “какова цель, порядок и господство этой любви?” Одно и то же благо может быть хлебом для пути к Богу или кирпичом для Вавилона сердца.

### 2.3. Predominant worldliness vs mortified remnants in believers

Baxter gives one of the most useful distinctions for the whole series:

- **worldliness predominant** — sign of spiritual death;
- **worldliness mortified/subdued** — still sin, but consistent with saving grace.

This protects both sides:

- do not comfort a ruling worldliness as “normal weakness”;
- do not condemn every remaining inordinate desire in a believer as proof of no grace.

Article-ready sentence:

> Господствующая мирскость — знак смерти. Подавляемые остатки мирской любви — настоящий грех, но не обязательно доказательство отсутствия благодати. В живом человеке грех ещё остаётся, но он уже должен быть ненавидим, оплакиваем, отвергаем и подчиняем Христу.

### 2.4. Baxter’s signs of worldliness

Baxter’s list is extremely practical. Key signs to mine for the series:

- valuing fleshly prosperity above its worth;
- loving creatures not as helps to God’s service but as provision for the flesh;
- desiring more than is useful for duty;
- anxious cares about the future;
- discontent when having daily bread;
- worldly thoughts sweeter than thoughts of Christ, grace, and heaven;
- speech freer about worldly prosperity than about soul matters;
- worldly business crowding out serious duties;
- over-dejection under losses;
- being more thankful for outward gifts than spiritual helps;
- more concern for children’s worldly condition than their spiritual condition;
- unwillingness to give beyond trifles;
- using sinful means for gain.

Diagnostic grid:

| Test | Lawful comfort | Worldliness / idolatrous drift |
|---|---|---|
| End | used for God, service, gratitude, neighbour | used for flesh, pride, identity, status, security |
| Order | below God and eternal hope | above spiritual life, duty, and obedience |
| Thought-life | received with thanks, not ruling the mind | easier/sweeter thoughts of prosperity than Christ |
| Loss | grief, but not spiritual collapse | loss feels worse than sin or God’s displeasure |
| Speech | can speak of it gratefully | freer/sweeter speech about comfort than God/soul |
| Use | daily bread, service, generosity | hoarding, pampering flesh, sinful means |

## 3. David Clarkson — soul-idolatry as inward worship of the creature

**Primary source:** David Clarkson, *Soul Idolatry Excludes Men Out of Heaven*.

Source anchors:

- Digital Puritan PDF: https://www.digitalpuritan.net/Digital%20Puritan%20Resources/Clarkson%2C%20David/Works%20%28vol.2%29%20Ind%20Titles/%5BDC%5D%20Soul%20Idolatry%20Excludes%20Men%20Out%20of%20Heaven.pdf
- Monergism HTML: https://www.monergism.com/soul-idolatry-excludes-men-out-heaven

Clarkson is one of the strongest sources for inward idolatry. But he must be used carefully: his force is not “every affection = idol”; his force is that inward acts of worship can be given to creatures.

### 3.1. Trust, fear, dependence

Clarkson’s framework is crucial: idolatry is not only external bowing to images. The soul can give inward worship to creatures by:

- trusting them;
- fearing them supremely;
- depending on them;
- loving them above God;
- seeking rest/felicity in them;
- serving them as ruling lusts.

Especially useful: trust in means, instruments, wealth, friends, self-wisdom, parts/accomplishments can become idolatrous when they receive dependence due to God.

Article-ready sentence:

> Clarkson помогает назвать идола не всякое дорогое сердцу благо, а то, чему душа отдаёт акты поклонения: высшее доверие, страх, зависимость, покой, служение, надежду и подчинение.

### 3.2. The “carnal man’s trinity”

Clarkson’s classic diagnostic cluster:

```text
pleasures — riches — honours
lust of the flesh — lust of the eyes — pride of life
comfort — security — status
```

This is useful, but must not be flattened into simplistic accusations. Not every pleasure is idolatry, not every money concern is covetousness, not every honour is pride. The issue is reigning love and worship.

Pastoral use:

- pleasure can be a gift, but becomes god when comfort rules obedience;
- riches can be tools, but become god when they receive trust;
- honour can be stewardship, but becomes god when reputation governs conscience.

## 4. John Flavel — prosperity and “creature-comforts” as special danger

**Primary source:** John Flavel, *On Keeping the Heart / A Saint Indeed*.

Source anchors:

- CCEL PDF: https://www.ccel.org/f/flavel/keeping/cache/keeping.pdf
- Monergism PDF: https://www.monergism.com/thethreshold/sdg/flavel/Keeping%20the%20Heart%20-%20John%20Flavel.pdf

Flavel gives a concrete case: how a Christian may keep his heart from pride and carnal security under the smiles of Providence and the confluence of creature-comforts.

This is gold because it refuses two errors:

1. prosperity is not automatically sin;
2. prosperity is spiritually dangerous because the heart becomes secure, proud, and earthly.

Article-ready formulation:

> Flavel не говорит, что Божьи улыбки провидения злы. Он говорит, что сердце в них особенно опасно: оно легко становится земным, самодовольным и безопасным без Бога. Поэтому благословение требует не подозрительности к Богу, а особой стражи над сердцем.

### 4.1. Practical diagnostic

When comfort increases, ask:

- Does gratitude increase or entitlement?
- Does prayer deepen or become unnecessary?
- Does giving widen or tighten?
- Does the heart become more tender or more self-secure?
- Does comfort free me for service or domesticate me into complacency?
- Is Christ still the treasure, or has providence become the treasure?

## 5. Jeremiah Burroughs — Moses’ choice and self-denial

**Primary source:** Jeremiah Burroughs, *Moses his Choice with his Eye Fixed upon Heaven* (1650), EEBO/TCP.

Source anchor:

- EEBO/TCP item: https://quod.lib.umich.edu/e/eebo/A30592.0001.001?view=toc

Burroughs is useful not because he gives a neat modern “idol chart,” but because he shows the **positive beauty of a self-denying choice**. Moses refuses the pleasures of sin and chooses affliction with the people of God because he has another treasure in view.

This helps the article avoid merely negative language. The heart does not overcome worldly love only by hating the world in abstraction. It overcomes by a better sight, a better treasure, a better hope.

Article-ready sentence:

> Самоотречение у Burroughs не является презрением к бытию и радости. Это выбор лучшего сокровища: сердце способно отказаться от меньшей сладости не потому, что стало бесчеловечным, а потому что увидело большую славу.

## 6. Thomas Watson — godliness is solid, not airy; Christ must be prized

**Primary source:** Thomas Watson, *The Godly Man’s Picture*.

Source anchors:

- Monergism PDF: https://www.monergism.com/thethreshold/sdg/watson/The%20Godly%20Man%27s%20Picture%20-%20Thomas%20Watson.pdf
- IA scan listing: https://archive.org/details/bim_eighteenth-century_the-godlymans-picture-_watson-thomas_1758

Watson describes godliness as solid and heart-taking; he also gives marks such as knowledge, faith, love, likeness to God, careful worship, serving God not men, and prizing Christ.

Use Watson carefully: marks help expose pretence, but marks must not replace Christ. His value here is in showing that real godliness has a changed valuation: Christ becomes precious above worldly prizes.

Article-ready sentence:

> В истинной благодати меняется не только поведение, но оценка: сердце начинает “ценить” Христа выше того, что раньше давало безопасность, сладость, статус или смысл.

## 7. Richard Baxter — Crucifying of the World: love of God + self-denial

**Primary source:** Richard Baxter, *The Crucifying of the World by the Cross of Christ*.

Source anchor:

- Monergism PDF: https://www.monergism.com/thethreshold/sdg/baxter/The%20Crucifying%20of%20the%20World%20by%20-%20Richard%20Baxter.pdf

Baxter’s opening is highly useful: he takes the love of God and self-denial as the sum of saving grace and religion — love of God as the positive part, self-denial as the opposing/negative part.

This gives the article a high-level frame:

```text
Saving grace turns the heart positively to God in love.
Saving grace turns the heart negatively away from self as lord.
Worldliness is not defeated by bare austerity, but by cross-shaped love and self-denial.
```

Diagnostic use:

- A person can contend for “true doctrine” while still being governed by selfish will, worldly honour, and carnal interest.
- Orthodoxy is not automatically freedom from worldliness.
- Real piety shows itself in love to God and crucified self-rule.

## 8. Final pastoral map: not every comfort is an idol

| Case | What is happening | Wrong response | Better response |
|---|---|---|---|
| Lawful enjoyment | gift received from God with gratitude and service | accuse every joy as idolatry | teach thanksgiving and ordered love |
| Creature-help | money/health/friends used as means under God | despise means as unspiritual | use means without trusting them as God |
| Inordinate attachment | comfort begins to dominate thought, fear, and choices | instantly declare apostasy | call to watchfulness, repentance, re-ordering |
| Reigning worldliness | creature becomes chief good/security/felicity | comfort as mere weakness | expose as death-sign and call to Christ |
| Weak believer with remnants | grieves and fights worldly love | crush as hypocrite | comfort + mortification + means of grace |
| Prosperous believer | blessed by providence but spiritually vulnerable | condemn prosperity itself | warn against security, pride, earthly drift |

## 9. Article module: “When does a gift become an idol?”

A gift becomes an idol not when it is loved, but when it is loved out of order. Baxter is crucial here: creatures may be loved as God’s works, as helps to service, as daily bread for the journey to heaven, and as means for doing good. The problem begins when the gift is sought more for the flesh than for God, more for pride than service, more for temporal felicity than eternal hope.

Clarkson presses the deeper issue: idolatry is an inward act of worship. The soul may bow without the body bowing. It bows when it gives the creature its highest trust, fear, rest, dependence, obedience, or hope. Flavel adds that prosperity is especially dangerous because the heart can grow secure under the smiles of providence. Burroughs shows the positive cure: the heart must see a better treasure. Watson reminds us that godliness changes what the soul prizes. Baxter’s *Crucifying of the World* ties it together: saving religion is love to God and self-denial through the cross of Christ.

Therefore the article should avoid both simplifications:

```text
Not: “All comfort is idolatry.”
Not: “Comfort is harmless if it is technically lawful.”
But: “Lawful comfort becomes a snare when it becomes ruling comfort.”
```

A mature pastoral question is not merely, “Do you enjoy this?” but:

```text
Can you receive it with thanks?
Can you lose it without losing God?
Can you use it for service?
Can you refuse it for obedience?
Does it lead your heart upward to the Giver?
Or has it become your sweetness, safety, status, and rest?
```

## 11. Best short formula for the series

```text
Lawful comfort: gift under God.
Worldliness: gift above God.
Soul-idolatry: creature receiving inward worship.
Prosperity: not sin, but dangerous weather for the heart.
Self-denial: not hatred of joy, but choosing the better treasure.
Weak grace: fights disordered love.
Dead worldliness: rests in disordered love.
```
