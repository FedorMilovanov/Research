## 1. Главный вывод V67

V66 закрепил Boston как центральную карту. V67 углубляет не названием новых авторов, а **проверкой богословских механизмов внутри карты**:

```text
Boston = fourfold-map.
1689 / WCF / Savoy = confessional skeleton of the will in four states.
Vincent = catechetical bridge: nature / grace / glory + ordinary means.
Manton = pastoral-theological bridge: state altered, grace vs glory, visible means vs inward kingdom.
Charnock = regeneration anatomy: two states, no middle, real change, grace predominating but corruption still opposing.
Flavel = gospel ministry as appointed external means, not self-repair.
```

**Итог:** схема четырёх состояний не должна остаться абстрактной таблицей. Её надо превратить в диагностическую грамматику:

```text
state of innocency: created upright, mutable
state of sin/nature: spiritually unable, not self-converting
state of grace: renewed, real new principle, yet remaining corruption
state of glory: perfectly and immutably free to good alone
```

## 2. Confessional parallel: WCF / Savoy / 1689 are nearly the same four-state grammar

### Primary locators checked

- Westminster Confession of Faith, Chapter 9, `Of Free Will`: https://www.fpchurch.org.uk/about-us/important-documents/the-westminster-confession-of-faith/
- 1689 London Baptist Confession, Chapter 9: https://www.bible-researcher.com/1689/chapter9.html
- 1689 Chapter 9 in modern page layout: https://www.the1689confession.com/1689/chapter-9
- Savoy Declaration, Chapter 9: https://www.the-highway.com/savoy_declaration.html
- Parallel table WCF / Savoy / 1689: https://www.proginosko.com/docs/wcf_sdfo_lbcf.html

### What this adds beyond V66

The confessional tradition is not merely saying “man has free will”. It traces the **will according to state**:

```text
innocency: able and free to will good, but mutable
sin: wholly lost ability to spiritual good accompanying salvation
conversion/grace: freed from natural bondage under sin, enabled to will spiritual good
remaining corruption: still not perfectly nor only willing good
glory: perfectly and immutably free to good alone
```

### Editorial use

Do not frame the article as if Boston invented a private scheme. Better:

> Boston gives the most convenient pastoral map; the confessions give the same grammar in the doctrine of the will. Man’s will must be interpreted according to his state: innocence, sin, grace, or glory.

### Baptist-site advantage

Because 1689 Chapter 9 has the same state-grammar, the article can remain explicitly Baptist/Reformed:

> В 1689 London Baptist Confession четыре состояния проступают через главу о свободной воле: невинность, грех, благодать и слава. Это не чужая импортированная схема, а часть баптистско-реформатской антропологии.

## 3. Thomas Vincent — catechetical precision for ordinary readers

### Primary locator

- Thomas Vincent, `The Shorter Catechism Explained from Scripture` PDF:  
  https://www.monergism.com/thethreshold/sdg/vincent/The%20Shorter%20Catechism%20-%20Thomas%20Vincent.pdf
- EEBO bibliographic record, 1675 edition:  
  https://quod.lib.umich.edu/e/eebo/A64986.0001.001?view=toc

### Important anchors found

1. Vincent distinguishes freedoms of the will:

```text
will free only to good = God, angels, glorified saints
will free only unto sin = devil and mankind in state of nature
will free both to good and evil = regenerate persons, because grace is real but corruption remains
```

2. He repeats the covenant-of-grace movement:

```text
estate of sin and misery → estate of salvation by a Redeemer
```

3. He explains effectual calling as Spirit-work on mind and will:

```text
convincing of sin and misery
illumination in the knowledge of Christ
renewing the will
persuading and enabling to embrace Christ
```

4. He treats ordinances as ordinary means:

```text
God can extraordinarily bring a person out of state of nature into state of grace,
but Word / sacraments / prayer are the usual appointed means.
```

### Why Vincent is gold

Vincent is extremely useful because he translates the whole discussion into catechetical language. For article readers, this is clearer than beginning with technical scholastic terms.

### Article-ready use

> Thomas Vincent helps make the map pastoral: in the state of nature the will is not forced by external compulsion, yet it is morally bound to sin; in the state of grace the will is truly renewed, but not yet perfected; in glory alone it is immutably free to good alone.

### Guardrail

Do not use Vincent to say “believers can choose good and evil equally”. His point is not neutrality. His point is that the regenerate will has a real principle of grace, while remaining corruption still disturbs it.

## 4. Thomas Manton — state altered, inward kingdom, grace vs glory

### Primary locators checked

- Thomas Manton, `Complete Works`, Vol. I, CCEL PDF:  
  https://www.ccel.org/ccel/m/manton/manton01/cache/manton01.pdf
- Thomas Manton, CCEL / Digital Puritan corpus locators found via searchable PDFs.

### Important anchors found in Vol. I

#### 4.1. Justification / pardon changes the state of the person

Manton says that when a sinner repents and believes, the state of the person is altered: from a state of wrath to a state of grace, from child of the devil to child of God.

**Use:** this is a strong bridge from legal/pardon language to state-language.

#### 4.2. Kingdom internally received differs from glory by degree

Manton distinguishes the kingdom externally administered in ordinances from the kingdom internally received as grace ruling in the hearts of the elect; he then says this differs from the kingdom of glory not so much in nature as in degree.

**Use:** very useful for explaining continuity between grace and glory without confusing them.

```text
state of grace = true beginning of the kingdom within
state of glory = same kingdom consummated, perfected
```

#### 4.3. Christ as beginning of new creation

Manton links Christ’s title “the beginning” to the renewed estate / new creation, and distinguishes life of grace and life of glory.

**Use:** keeps the state of grace Christological, not psychological.

### Article-ready paragraph

> Manton helps prevent two mistakes at once. Grace is not merely outward church privilege, because the kingdom must be internally received and rule in the heart. But grace is also not yet glory: the inward kingdom is real now, while its perfected form belongs to the state of glory.

## 5. Stephen Charnock — strongest V67 diagnostic addition

### Primary locator

- Stephen Charnock, `Complete Works`, Vol. 3, Digital Puritan full text:  
  https://www.digitalpuritan.net/Digital%20Puritan%20Resources/Charnock%2C%20Stephen/The%20Complete%20Works%20of%20Stephen%20Charnock%20%28vol.3%29.txt.html

### 5.1. Two-state diagnostic inside the four-state map

Charnock says there are but two states in the immediate saving/damning sense:

```text
state of sin / death / enmity / darkness
state of righteousness / life / friendship / light
```

He explicitly rejects a middle saving category:

```text
no medium between these states
beautiful moralist without faith remains in enmity
weak believer with faith is in amity/life
```

### Why this matters

Boston’s four states include creation and eternity. Charnock’s two-state diagnostic operates inside history after the fall:

```text
fallen mankind is either still in nature/death or brought into grace/life
```

This prevents vague “almost Christian” categories from replacing conversion.

### 5.2. Regeneration vs conversion

Charnock distinguishes:

```text
regeneration = spiritual change / principle / power conferred
conversion = spiritual motion / exercise of that power
life precedes motion
man is passive in regeneration, active in conversion after being revived
```

This is extremely important for the article because it answers the discipline/self-repair problem.

### 5.3. State of grace = predominancy of grace, not absence of all corruption

Charnock says the state of nature consists in the prevalency of the corrupt habit, while the state of grace consists in the predominancy of the gracious habit, which spreads over the whole soul while striving with the powerful opposite that still partly resides there.

This is one of the best V67 finds.

```text
state of nature = corrupt habit prevails over the whole man
state of grace = gracious habit predominates over the soul
remaining corruption = powerful opposite still partly resides there
state of glory = no such remaining opposition
```

### 5.4. Habit and custom: why “just discipline” is not enough

Charnock’s discussion of habit is very important for the user’s earlier concern about patterns, childhood formation, and deeply rooted attachments. He argues that custom can deepen nature’s disorder; a person does not cure sinful habit by mere self-accustoming, because the heart itself needs overruling grace.

### Article-ready paragraph

> Charnock gives one of the cleanest pastoral formulations: the difference between nature and grace is not that the believer has no remaining corruption, but that grace has become the predominant living principle. In nature the corrupt habit rules; in grace a new principle rules and fights the opposite that still partly remains.

## 6. Flavel — external means and the state of enmity

### Primary locators checked

- John Flavel, `Method of Grace`, CCEL HTML:  
  https://ccel.org/ccel/flavel/grace/grace.vi.html
- Google Books bibliographic locator, 1681 `Method of Grace`:  
  https://books.google.com/books/about/Method_of_Grace.html?id=ZXw7AAAAcAAJ

### V67 addition

Flavel states that gospel ministers are ambassadors appointed to reconcile sinners to God, and that sinful, rebellious hearts naturally have a strong aversion from God. He also says sinners cannot receive the benefits purchased by Christ while remaining in the state of enmity and alienation.

### Use

Flavel is excellent for the **means-of-grace** paragraph:

```text
external call and ministry are appointed means
natural heart is averse
saving efficacy is not in the preacher’s power
benefits of Christ must be received through reconciliation/application
```

### Guardrail

This protects against both errors:

- “Just fix your heart yourself” — no, the natural heart is in enmity.
- “Means do nothing” — no, gospel ministry is Christ’s appointed means.

## 9. Article section blueprint after V67

### Section title

**Четыре состояния человека: почему сердце нельзя лечить одним языком**

### Movement

1. **Созданное сердце** — created upright, mutable, not evil by nature.
2. **Падшее сердце** — not merely undisciplined, but spiritually unable and in enmity.
3. **Возрождённое сердце** — not perfected, but truly renewed; grace predominates while corruption remains and fights.
4. **Прославленное сердце** — finally and immutably free to good alone.

### Bridge paragraph

> Поэтому нельзя говорить о сердце одинаково во всех состояниях. Невозрождённому человеку нужна не техника духовной дисциплины, а Христос и новое рождение. Возрождённому человеку нужна не паника из-за всякого остаточного греха, а трезвое умерщвление греха и жизнь из нового принципа благодати. А полной цельности сердца нельзя требовать до славы: это не нынешнее состояние благодати, а обетованное завершение.

## 11. Pastoral guardrails strengthened by V67

1. **Against moralism:** Charnock + Vincent show that nature is not cured by self-discipline.
2. **Against perfectionism:** 1689 + Vincent + Charnock show remaining corruption in the state of grace.
3. **Against despair:** Manton and Charnock show weak faith may still be real grace.
4. **Against cheap assurance:** Charnock’s no-middle-state language forbids treating morality without faith as saving.
5. **Against anti-means mysticism:** Flavel and Vincent preserve Word / ordinances / prayer as appointed ordinary means.
6. **Against sacramental/external formalism:** Manton distinguishes outward administration from inwardly received kingdom.
