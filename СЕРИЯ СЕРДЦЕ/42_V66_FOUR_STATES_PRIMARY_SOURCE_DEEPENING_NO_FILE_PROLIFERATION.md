## 1. Главный вывод V66

На уровне первичных текстов нужно различать три слоя:

1. **Boston** — единственный центральный источник, который прямо строит всю карту `fourfold state` как самостоятельную практическую антропологию.
2. **Confessional Baptist/Reformed layer** — Westminster / Savoy / 1689 / Baptist Catechism дают тот же богословский каркас в форме: creation/innocency → state of sin → state of grace → state of glory/salvation.
3. **Puritan pastoral layer** — Owen, Flavel, Goodwin, Sibbes, Brooks, Ames, Baxter не всегда дают ровно “четыре состояния” в виде одной схемы, но дают отдельные золотые узлы: перевод из nature в grace, различение остаточного греха и господства греха, состояние славы, подготовка/убеждение совести, опасность ложного мира.

**Редакционное правило:** не заставлять каждого пуританина говорить “fourfold state”. Формулировать аккуратно:

> У Boston карта дана прямо; у других пуритан мы находим не копию схемы, а те же богословские переходы и пастырские различения: nature → grace → glory, sin remaining vs sin reigning, state of sin and misery → state of salvation.

## 2. Boston — центр карты, а не просто один из свидетелей

Проверенные первичные локаторы:

- Internet Archive, 1772: `Human nature in its fourfold state; or, the newest whole duty of man... By Mr. Thomas Boston`  
  https://archive.org/details/bim_eighteenth-century_human-nature-in-its-four_boston-thomas_1772
- Internet Archive, 1784 Falkirk edition, with Robert Wightman preface:  
  https://archive.org/details/humannatureinits02bost
- Open Library work page lists the older edition line and many editions, including first American edition 1796:  
  https://openlibrary.org/works/OL1135399W/Human_nature_in_its_four-fold_state_of_primitive_integrity_entire_depravation_begun_recovery_and_con
- HathiTrust catalogue confirms title formula: `primitive integrity, entire depravation, begun recovery, and consummate happiness or misery`:  
  https://catalog.hathitrust.org/Record/008632107

### 2.1. Editorial use

Boston should be the **structural backbone** for any article section on the four states:

```text
State I: primitive integrity / innocence
State II: entire depravation / nature / sin
State III: begun recovery / grace
State IV: consummate happiness or misery / eternal state
```

### 2.2. Why this matters for “heart” series

Boston prevents two opposite pastoral failures:

1. **Moralism toward the unregenerate:** treating the state of nature as if it were only weak discipline.
2. **Crushing the regenerate:** treating remaining sin as if it automatically proved the continued dominion of sin.

Use Boston not only for a title, but for the whole diagnostic sequence:

```text
created upright → fallen and unable → regenerated and fighting → finally fixed in glory or misery
```

## 3. 1689 London Baptist Confession — Baptist-compatible four-state skeleton

Primary/confessional locator:

- The1689.org, Chapter 9 `Of Free Will`: https://the1689.org/chapters/9
- Bridport Baptist Church text of 1689, Chapter 9 and 10: https://bridportbaptistchurch.org/second-london-baptist-confession

Chapter 9 gives a very clean sequence:

```text
9.2 state of innocency
9.3 fall into a state of sin
9.4 translated into a state of grace
9.5 state of glory only = perfectly and immutably free to good alone
```

Chapter 10 adds the heart-change language:

```text
out of state of sin and death → to grace and salvation
mind enlightened → heart of stone taken away → heart of flesh given → will renewed
```

### 3.1. Editorial use for Baptist site

For your site this is a very important anchor because it keeps the article from sounding like “Presbyterian-only Boston terminology”. The language of 1689 gives the same four-state structure in a Particular Baptist confessional grammar.

Recommended formula:

> В баптистско-реформатской традиции эта карта не чужая: 1689 London Baptist Confession в главе о свободной воле проходит через состояние невинности, состояние греха, состояние благодати и состояние славы.

## 4. Baptist Catechism / Keach’s Catechism — catechetical bridge for readers

Primary-text locator:

- Digital Puritan full text, `The Baptist Catechism, commonly called Keach's Catechism`:  
  https://www.digitalpuritan.net/Digital%20Puritan%20Resources/Keach%2C%20Benjamin/Keach%27s%20Catechism.txt.html

Useful sequence:

```text
Q20: fall brought mankind into a state of sin and misery.
Q21: sinfulness of that state = guilt, want of original righteousness, corruption of whole nature, actual transgressions.
Q23: covenant of grace delivers out of state of sin and misery and brings into state of salvation by a Redeemer.
Q34: effectual calling = conviction, enlightening, renewing the will, persuading/enabling to embrace Christ.
```

### 4.1. Editorial use

Use for the “simple explanatory” paragraph after the deeper Boston/1689 block:

> Катехизисный язык говорит просто: падение ввело человека в состояние греха и бедствия; Бог в завете благодати выводит Своих из этого состояния к спасению через Искупителя.

This lets the article speak to ordinary readers without losing doctrinal precision.

## 5. John Owen — key distinction: rebellion of sin vs dominion of sin

Primary locator:

- CCEL PDF, John Owen, `A Treatise of the Dominion of Sin and Grace`, London 1688:  
  https://www.ccel.org/ccel/o/owen/sin_grace/cache/sin_grace.pdf
- CCEL text title page:  
  https://ccel.org/ccel/owen/sin_grace/sin_grace.i.i.html

Owen is not used as the “four-state author”. He is used as the **pastoral discriminator** inside state of grace.

Most important V66 finding:

```text
state of grace ≠ absence of sin
state of grace = sin no longer has dominion, although sin remains and fights
state of glory = absence of sin in the final sense
```

Owen is vital because he explicitly warns that freedom from dominion is not absolute freedom from all sin now. That protects weak believers.

### 5.1. Diagnostic formula

```text
sin remaining = can be consistent with state of grace
sin reigning = sign of dominion, incompatible with true gospel liberty
sin removed = state of glory, not the present state of grace
```

### 5.2. Article-ready paragraph

> Owen helps here with one of the most important pastoral distinctions: the question is not merely whether sin is present, but whether sin reigns. In believers sin still dwells and fights; in the state of grace it is dethroned, not annihilated. Absolute freedom from sin belongs not to grace on earth, but to glory.

Primary locator:

- CCEL, Flavel, `Method of Grace in the Gospel Redemption`:  
  https://ccel.org/ccel/flavel/grace/grace.xx.html

Flavel’s value: he directly names the movement:

```text
sad state of nature → blessed privileged state of grace
```

### 6.1. Editorial use

Flavel can support the Christ-centered transition:

> Переход из природы в благодать не является самоперевоспитанием. Это перевод, купленный и удерживаемый Христом.

This is important so the series does not become “heart technique” instead of gospel anthropology.

## 7. Thomas Goodwin — state of glory as pastoral horizon

Primary locators:

- Monergism PDF, Goodwin, `Of the Blessed State of Glory Which the Saints Possess After Death`:  
  https://www.monergism.com/thethreshold/sdg/goodwin/Of%20the%20Blessed%20State%20of%20Glory%20W%20-%20Thomas%20Goodwin.pdf
- Digital Puritan text, Goodwin works vol. 7:  
  https://www.digitalpuritan.net/Digital%20Puritan%20Resources/Goodwin%2C%20Thomas/The%20Works%20of%20Thomas%20Goodwin%20%28vol.7%29.txt.html

Goodwin is not mainly for state of nature; he is gold for **state of glory** as a horizon that affects both:

```text
those in state of nature → warning / call to Christ
those in state of grace → loosening from worldliness / heavenly-mindedness
```

### 7.1. Editorial use

Goodwin gives a strong closing horizon for the article:

> Вечная слава — не декоративная концовка антропологии. Для человека в природе она обличает привязанность ко греху; для человека в благодати она учит жить как человек другого мира.

## 8. Richard Sibbes — “wholly” vs “in part”: careful language about remaining corruption

Primary/near-primary locators:

- A Puritan’s Mind text, Sibbes, `The Church’s Complaint and Confidence`:  
  https://www.apuritansmind.com/puritan-favorites/puritan-favorites-richard-sibbes-1577-1635/the-churchs-complaint-and-confidence-a-sermon-by-richard-sibbes/
- Monergism PDF, Sibbes, `The Life of Faith` modernized; use only as locator / not as final quotation base if exact seventeenth-century wording is required:  
  https://www.monergism.com/thethreshold/sdg/sibbes/The%20Life%20of%20Faith%20%28Modernized%29%20-%20Richard%20Sibbes.pdf

Sibbes helps explain that the wicked are under sin “wholly”, while the best still have corruption “in part”. This is a very useful pastoral nuance.

### 8.1. Editorial use

> В человеке природы грех властвует как состояние; в верующем остаточная нечистота остаётся “в части”, но она уже не определяет его целиком как старое состояние.

This helps avoid the crude sentence: “If there is still corruption, nothing changed.”

## 9. William Ames — useful but dangerous if used without confessional guardrails

Primary/secondary locator status:

- EEBO text of `The Marrow of Sacred Divinity`: https://quod.lib.umich.edu/e/eebo/A25291.0001.001/
- CPRC article gives references to Ames, `Conscience With the Power and Cases Thereof`, Book II, Chapter 4, pp. 8–9, but the article itself is secondary and critical:  
  https://cprc.co.uk/articles/preparationism/

Ames is useful because he speaks of moving a person:

```text
out of a state of sin → into a state of grace
```

But Ames must be handled carefully because “preparation for conversion” can be misunderstood as semi-Pelagian if detached from total inability and effectual calling.

### 9.1. Editorial guardrail

Never write:

> The sinner prepares himself into grace.

Write instead:

> In Puritan pastoral casuistry there is attention to conviction, humiliation, and awakening before conversion; but the transition from spiritual death to life remains the work of sovereign grace, not self-generated preparation.

## 10. Brooks / Sibbes / assurance: state of grace is not the same as sense of grace

A separate pastoral distinction should be inserted into the series:

```text
state of grace = objective saving condition before God
sense/assurance of grace = subjective awareness, which may be weak, clouded, assaulted, or recovering
```

This distinction matters because a tender conscience may lack assurance and wrongly conclude: “I am not in grace.” The article must not collapse the state itself into the felt sense of the state.

### 10.1. Pastoral use

Ask:

```text
Does the person lack grace, or lack assurance?
Is the person defending sin, or grieving sin?
Is there hatred of sin as sin, or only fear of consequences?
Is there flight to Christ, even weakly?
```

## 11. Final V66 synthesis for the article

Use this as the backbone paragraph:

> Пуританская и реформатская антропология различает не просто типы темперамента, а состояния человека перед Богом. Человек был создан в состоянии невинности; через падение вошёл в состояние греха и бедствия; через действенное призвание и соединение со Христом переводится в состояние благодати; и в конце будет утверждён в состоянии славы или оставлен в окончательном суде. Поэтому пастырская диагностика сердца должна спрашивать не только “какая привычка?”, “какая травма?”, “какой идол?”, но и “в каком состоянии перед Богом находится человек?”. Невозрождённому нужен не просто навык, а Христос и новое рождение. Верующему нужно не отчаяние от остаточного греха, а различение: грех остаётся и воюет, но не должен царствовать. А полная свобода от греха принадлежит не сегодняшней слабой благодати, а будущей славе.
