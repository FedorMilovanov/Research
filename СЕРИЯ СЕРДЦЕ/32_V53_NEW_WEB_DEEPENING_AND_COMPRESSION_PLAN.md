The previous v52 archive had 495 files total, including 354 Markdown files. That was useful during discovery but bad for writing: the same themes were split into dozens of fragments. v53 therefore does **two things at once**:

1. preserves the full content of the old archive under original file headings;

## 2. New web-deepening findings added in v53

### 2.1. Ordinary-day godliness was still underdeveloped

The archive had many crisis themes: temptation, self-deception, assurance, suffering. It needed more material on the heart in ordinary daily practice. Thomas Gouge’s *Christian Directions, shewing how to walk with God all the day long* is an important locator because it is explicitly framed as daily walking with God, not just emergency counsel. EEBO/TCP has the text and title metadata.

**Use in article:** show that the heart is formed not only in dramatic falls but also in morning, work, family, speech, money, prayer, fatigue, and ordinary routines.

**Quote status:** locator-only until exact section is opened.

### 2.2. Household/family heart-patterns need a separate layer

William Gouge’s *Of Domesticall Duties* gives a large Puritan structure for household relationships: spouses, children, parents, masters, servants, all under Ephesians 5:21–6:9. This is useful because many heart patterns are learned, exposed, and sanctified in ordinary relationships, not only private introspection.

**Guardrail:** use Gouge carefully. Do not import every historical social assumption uncritically; use him as a source on practical godliness in household relations, not as a final modern abuse/safety protocol.

### 2.3. Seasons of heart-keeping need sharper structure

Flavel’s *Keeping the Heart* is not only a general call to guard the heart. It structures special seasons: prosperity, adversity, temptation, spiritual darkness, public danger, sickness, and other critical times. This strengthens the series because different seasons expose different heart-dangers: pride in prosperity, murmuring in adversity, unbelief in darkness, false security in peace.

**Use in article:** create a “seasons of the heart” module.

### 2.4. Covenant and law written in the heart should not be a side-note

John Ball’s *Treatise of the Covenant of Grace* explicitly connects the law written in the heart of the spiritual seed with the covenant of grace. This helps keep the series from sounding like self-improvement: obedience flows from grace and covenantal renewal, not bare moral pressure.

### 2.5. Burgess should become a major assurance/signs source, not a footnote

Anthony Burgess’s *Spiritual Refining* is not merely a source for “assurance.” Its title/subtitle maps a whole cluster: doctrine of assurance, use of signs in self-examination, true grace vs counterfeit, true and false signs, regeneration, new creature, heart of flesh, vocation, sanctification, and cases of conscience. This should be one of the main sources for the religious-heart and assurance sections.
