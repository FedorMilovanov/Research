# V57 — Conservative Source Screening & Further Deepening

Status: **single ZIP continuation after v56**  
Purpose: continue research while keeping the archive compact and conservative in theological posture.

## 0. Summary

V57 does not add a mass of new topical files. It adds one consolidated screening layer that answers the user's concern:

> Search further, but keep the theological center conservative: Puritans, Reformed/Baptist confessional sources, TMS/MacArthur orbit where useful, D. A. Carson/TGC carefully, Calvin, and similar sources. Check whether any discovered source is liberal, anti-inerrancy, or rejects Scripture as final authority. Secular sources may remain only for psychological/medical terminology and safety guardrails.

### Main decision

No source should be treated as a doctrinal anchor unless it passes the **Scripture Authority Filter**:

1. affirms Scripture as inspired, authoritative, truthful/inerrant or infallible;
2. does not subordinate Scripture to autonomous psychology, sociology, historical criticism, or experience;
3. does not redefine sin only as pathology, trauma, oppression, or social conditioning;
4. does not deny the need for repentance, faith, regeneration, sanctification, and final accountability before God;
5. is used within its proper lane: primary source, confession, biblical-theological aid, pastoral aid, historical locator, or secular auxiliary.

## 1. Updated source taxonomy

### A. Tier GREEN-1 — doctrinal anchors

Use first for doctrine of the heart, sin, repentance, sanctification, assurance, church, and pastoral care.

- Scripture.
- 1689 London Baptist Confession, Westminster Standards, Canons of Dort, Heidelberg Catechism.
- Reformation and Reformed/Puritan primary texts: Calvin, Owen, Charnock, Boston, Edwards, Sibbes, Goodwin, Brooks, Watson, Flavel, Baxter, Perkins, Ames, Burgess, Downame, Bridge, Manton, Bunyan, Burroughs, Marshall, Ball, etc.
- Confessionally Reformed / Reformed Baptist institutions when used for doctrinal framing: Founders, PRTS, RTS, WTS, SBTS Abstract where aligned with Scripture authority.

### B. Tier GREEN-2 — conservative evangelical / biblical-counseling aids

Use as secondary support, not as replacement for exegesis and primary theological sources.

- TMS / TMU / MacArthur / GTY: strong on inerrancy, sufficiency, expository preaching, literal-grammatical-historical hermeneutics, biblical counseling.
- Abner Chou: useful for hermeneutical method and continuity of biblical writers.
- Greg Gifford: useful for heart/habits and biblical counseling, under Scripture.
- CCEF, ACBC, Biblical Counseling Coalition: useful for counseling categories, fear of man, idols, habits, trauma caution, abuse reporting, but still under Scripture and confessional guardrails.
- 9Marks: useful for local church, membership, discipline, and warnings about misuse of discipline.
- Ligonier / R. C. Sproul: useful for Scripture authority, Reformed theology, bondage of the will, holiness, providence.
- D. A. Carson / TGC: useful for biblical theology, Scripture authority, exegetical essays; not a Baptist confession, not a final doctrinal court.

### C. Tier BLUE — repositories and access points, not authorities

These are not theologians. They are libraries or hosts. Their value depends on the text they host.

- Monergism.
- CCEL.
- Digital Puritan.
- EEBO/TCP / Michigan / Oxford Text Archive.
- Internet Archive.
- Google Books.
- Banner of Truth / Reformation Heritage / Crossway pages when used as bibliographic or publisher locators.

Rule: cite the author/work, not merely the repository as if the repository itself taught the doctrine.

### D. Tier YELLOW — useful but cautious

Can be used, but only with lane restrictions.

- TGC broadly: conservative evangelical but multi-author; Carson is stronger as a named source than generic TGC.
- Desiring God / Piper: broadly conservative/Reformed Baptist-friendly, but use as devotional/secondary, not confessional baseline.
- Modern publisher/blog descriptions: only for locating books, editions, or summary orientation.
- Bonhoeffer: keep as `CAUTION / illustration only`; do not use as confessional or Baptist/Reformed doctrinal anchor.

### E. Tier SILVER — secular auxiliary only

These sources may remain because the user explicitly allowed secular sources for psychological/medical terminology and safety.

- CDC, WHO, NIMH, SAMHSA, NCTSN, NASEM.
- Habit research and trauma literature.

Rule: these sources can define public-health/safety terms and remind us about risk, crisis, trauma effects, and safeguarding. They must not define sin, heart, repentance, regeneration, sanctification, or pastoral authority.

### F. Tier RED — exclude as theological anchors

Do not use as a source for doctrine of the heart, sin, Scripture, Christ, salvation, sanctification, church, or pastoral practice:

- liberal/critical theologians who reject or functionally relativize Scripture's authority/inerrancy;
- sources that treat the Bible primarily as errant religious literature;
- sources that redefine sin only as psychology, society, trauma, power, or self-expression;
- sources that deny substitutionary atonement, regeneration, repentance, final judgment, or Christ's uniqueness;
- sources that use higher-critical assumptions to overrule the biblical text.

## 4. Sources to keep but restrict

### 4.1. Secular trauma / health / safety sources

Keep because they serve safety and terminology.

Permitted use:

- suicide warning signs;
- abuse risk and reporting realism;
- trauma-informed caution against rough handling;
- habit formation as an illustrative analogy;
- social isolation and health risks as common-grace observation.

Forbidden use:

- redefining sin as only trauma;
- making clinical categories superior to Scripture;
- replacing repentance and faith with technique;
- letting secular anthropology set the doctrine of man.

### 4.2. Book blurbs and publisher pages

Use only for:

- title confirmation;
- edition/location;
- topic scan;
- not as exact quotation bank unless the page itself contains the exact quote being used.

## 5. New “article source order” rule

For every serious claim in the article, follow this order:

1. **Biblical text**.
2. **Confessional frame** where relevant.
3. **Primary historical source**: Calvin / Puritan / Reformed orthodox / Edwards / etc.
4. **Modern conservative aid**: TMS / Carson / Ligonier / Founders / CCEF / ACBC / 9Marks.
5. **Secular auxiliary** only for safety/medical/public-health terminology.

If a claim cannot be supported at levels 1–3, it should not become a major doctrinal claim.

## 7. Practical blacklist / warning protocol

Before adding any new theological source, ask:

1. Does it affirm Scripture as final authority?
2. Does it affirm biblical sin, repentance, Christ, regeneration, sanctification?
3. Does it treat Scripture as true revelation or merely religious tradition?
4. Does it subordinate doctrine to therapy, politics, sociology, or critical theory?
5. Does it deny or weaken inerrancy/infallibility/authority?
6. Is the source being used for doctrine or only for a narrow external fact?

If the answer is unclear, label:

`CAUTION_REQUIRES_THEOLOGICAL_SCREENING_BEFORE_USE`

If the answer is negative, label:

`DO_NOT_USE_AS_THEOLOGICAL_SOURCE`
