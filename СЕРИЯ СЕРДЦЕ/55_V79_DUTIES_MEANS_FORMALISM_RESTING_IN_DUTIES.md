# V79 — Duties, Means of Grace, Formal Worship, Resting in Duties, and the Difference Between Living Use and Dead Reliance

**Purpose:** add a Puritan/Reformed diagnostic layer on **religious duties**, **means of grace**, **public and private worship**, **prayer**, **hearing the Word**, **resting in duties**, **formalism**, and **living communion with Christ through appointed means**.

This layer is needed because the heart-series must avoid two opposite errors:

```text
Error 1: Duties save, prove, or replace Christ.
Error 2: Because duties cannot save, they can be neglected.
```

The Puritan balance is sharper:

```text
Duties are not Saviour.
Duties are not nothing.
Duties are appointed means, not atoning foundations.
A formal heart rests in the act.
A living heart seeks God through the act.
```

Article-ready thesis:

> Пуритане не говорили: “молишься — значит, живой”. Но они и не говорили: “раз молитва не спасает, она не важна”. Обязанности не являются Христом; но Христос назначил их как дороги, по которым Он питает, обличает, утешает и укрепляет Своих.

---

## 1. Why this layer must come after conscience and temptation

V78 protected conscience from human tyranny and scrupulous overreach. V77 traced how temptation moves toward consent. V79 now asks:

```text
What does the heart do with holy duties themselves?
```

This is necessary because a religious person can misuse even prayer, fasting, reading, worship, confession, and self-examination.

Four dangerous confusions:

1. **Resting in duties** — using duties as the ground of acceptance.
2. **Rejecting duties** — claiming grace while despising appointed means.
3. **Formal performance** — doing the act while the heart is absent.
4. **Morbid measurement** — turning every duty into self-salvation or self-torment.

Pastoral grid:

```text
Dead formalism: duty without heart.
Legal reliance: duty as righteousness.
Antinomian neglect: Christ named, means despised.
Weak grace: duty mixed with distraction, yet mourned and brought to Christ.
Living use: duty as appointed meeting-place with God, through Christ, by the Spirit.
```

## 2. Thomas Shepard — duties cannot save, but must not be cast off

**Primary source:** Thomas Shepard, *The Sound Believer*.

Source anchors:

- Monergism page and excerpt: https://www.monergism.com/sound-believer-ebook
- ECCO / later edition locator: https://preview.wellcomecollection.org/works/fgwneg4p
- Online Books locator: https://onlinebooks.library.upenn.edu/webbin/book/lookupname?key=Shepard%2C+Thomas%2C+1605-1649

Shepard gives one of the most usable Puritan formulas for this topic. He warns that the insufficiency of duties must teach a soul **never to rest in duties**, yet he immediately guards against the opposite error: duties are not to be thrown away, but laid at Christ’s feet.

Core distinction:

```text
Bad works damn.
Good duties cannot save.
Therefore do not rest in duties.
But do not cast off duties.
Cast them down at Christ’s feet.
```

This is gold because it prevents both legalism and carelessness.

Article-ready sentence:

> Shepard режет глубже, чем обычный лозунг “не будь законником”. Он не говорит: “раз обязанности не спасают, оставь их”. Он говорит: не опирайся на них как на Спасителя, но не выбрасывай их как мусор; положи их к ногам Христа.

Diagnostic use:

```text
If the soul says, “I prayed, therefore God must accept me,” it rests in duty.
If the soul says, “Duties do not justify, therefore I need not pray,” it despises means.
If the soul says, “Lord, even my best duty needs Christ,” it is learning evangelical dependence.
```

## 3. Stephen Charnock — spiritual worship is not bare outward form

**Primary source:** Stephen Charnock, *A Discourse Upon Spiritual Worship*.

Source anchors:

- Digital Puritan PDF: https://www.digitalpuritan.net/Digital%20Puritan%20Resources/Charnock%2C%20Stephen/Works%20%28vol.1%29%20Ind%20Titles/%5BSC%5D%20A%20Discourse%20Upon%20Spiritual%20Worship.pdf
- Monergism page: https://www.monergism.com/discourse-spiritual-worship
- Grace eBooks volume locator: https://www.grace-ebooks.com/library/Stephen%20Charnock/SC-Discourses-On-The-Existence-and-Attributes-of-God-Vol-1.pdf

Charnock’s central contribution is that worship must be spiritual because God is Spirit. A duty may be outwardly correct and yet inwardly unspiritual. But Charnock also refuses a gloomy view of worship: evangelical worship should carry praise, joy, delight, faith, love, and access to God as reconciled Father.

Core distinction:

```text
Outward act alone is not spiritual worship.
Spiritual worship is heart-engaged worship before God.
Evangelical worship is not legal terror but filial approach through reconciliation.
```

Article-ready sentence:

> Charnock помогает отличить серьёзность от законнической мрачности. Духовное поклонение не значит “только внешне строго”; оно значит — сердце действительно приходит к Богу как к Богу, через Евангелие, с верой, любовью, благоговением и сыновней свободой.

Pastoral use:

```text
A sad, slave-like frame may reveal legal fear, not holy seriousness.
A careless cheerful frame may reveal levity, not gospel delight.
True gospel worship is reverent and free, serious and filial, humble and glad.
```

## 4. David Clarkson — public worship is not optional individual taste

**Primary source:** David Clarkson, *Public Worship to be Preferred Before Private*.

Source anchors:

- A Puritan’s Mind text: https://www.apuritansmind.com/puritan-worship/public-worship-to-be-preferred-before-private-by-david-clarkson-1622-1686/
- Practical Works PDF locator: https://www.digitalpuritan.net/Digital%20Puritan%20Resources/Clarkson%2C%20David/The%20Practical%20Works%20of%20David%20Clarkson%20%28vol.1%29.pdf

Clarkson gives a needed corrective to modern individualism. He does not despise private worship; he explicitly recognizes worship in the dwellings of Jacob. But he argues that public worship has a distinct divine preference.

Core distinction:

```text
Private worship is real and necessary.
Public worship is not thereby made optional or inferior.
God’s gathered ordinances are not merely a personal devotional preference.
```

Article-ready sentence:

> Clarkson не противопоставляет личную молитву собранию, будто одно свято, а другое пусто. Он говорит тоньше: Бог принимает поклонение в домах, но особо любит ворота Сиона. Поэтому “я духовен дома” не должно становиться оправданием презрения к собранной церкви.

Pastoral use:

```text
Legalism: “I am accepted because I attended.”
Individualism: “I need no gathered church.”
Living use: “Christ feeds His people through appointed public ordinances, and I come needy.”
```

## 5. John Flavel — means and evidences serve union with Christ, not the other way around

**Primary sources:** John Flavel, *The Method of Grace* and *Keeping the Heart*.

Source anchors:

- CCEL *Method of Grace*, chapter V: https://ccel.org/ccel/flavel/grace/grace.v.html
- PDF locator: https://biblesnet.com/john-flavel-the-method-of-grace-in-the-gospel-redemption.pdf
- *Keeping the Heart* PDF: https://www.ccel.org/f/flavel/keeping/cache/keeping.pdf

Flavel is useful because he speaks to multiple readers at once: the sincere believer, the powerless professor with only the name of Christ, the doubting soul, and the presumptuous soul. He shows that evidences and duties are useful, but they must be ordered around union with Christ.

Key pastoral distinction from *Method of Grace*:

```text
Some things belong to the essence of spiritual life.
Some things belong only to its well-being.
Joys, manifestations, and comfort may fail for a time.
Union with Christ does not therefore fail.
```

This protects weak believers from confusing loss of comfort with loss of life.

Article-ready sentence:

> Flavel даёт очень важный порядок: не всякая потеря радости — потеря жизни. Утешение, проявления, духовная свежесть могут временно снизиться; но если есть союз со Христом, сущность жизни не уничтожена.

Diagnostic use:

```text
Formal professor: means may be present, but no living union with Christ.
Doubting believer: comfort may be absent, but union may be real.
Presumptuous soul: refusal to examine evidences is dangerous.
Sincere believer: evidences should melt, confirm, and quicken, not replace Christ.
```

## 6. John Owen — prayer is duty, but also communion and delight

**Primary sources:** John Owen, *The Grace and Duty of Being Spiritually Minded* and *Of Communion with God*.

Source anchors:

- Monergism PDF: https://www.monergism.com/thethreshold/sdg/owen/The%20Grace%20and%20Duty%20of%20Being%20Spi%20-%20John%20Owen.pdf
- IA 1657 *Of Communion with God*: https://archive.org/details/bim_early-english-books-1641-1700_of-communion-with-god_owen-john_1657
- Digital Puritan Works vol.2: https://www.digitalpuritan.net/Digital%20Puritan%20Resources/Owen%2C%20John/The%20Works%20of%20John%20Owen%20%28vol.2%29%201826.pdf

Owen protects prayer from two distortions:

```text
Prayer as bare required performance.
Prayer as emotional self-confirmation.
```

In *Spiritually Minded*, he describes the believer who has had real communion with God in prayer as spiritually refreshed; believers do not pray only because duty and need require it, but because there is holy rest and delight in communion with God.

Article-ready sentence:

> Owen показывает, что молитва зрелого сердца не сводится к “я обязан” и не сводится к “мне нужно”. В молитве верующий ищет Бога Самого; обязанность становится местом общения, а не монетой для покупки принятия.

Pastoral use:

```text
Dead duty: “I performed prayer.”
Legal reliance: “My prayer makes me acceptable.”
Emotional idolatry: “Only sweet prayer proves I am safe.”
Living communion: “Through Christ, by the Spirit, I come to God Himself.”
```

## 7. Richard Baxter — practical theology teaches how to use helps and means

**Primary source:** Richard Baxter, *A Christian Directory*.

Source anchor:

- EEBO/TCP: https://quod.lib.umich.edu/e/eebo/A26892.0001.001?view=toc

Baxter’s title itself is useful: a practical theology directing Christians how to use knowledge and faith, improve helps and means, perform duties, overcome temptations, and mortify sin. Baxter thus prevents an anti-practical version of grace.

Core distinction:

```text
Grace does not make means needless.
Means do not make grace mechanical.
The Christian life is ordered, directed, instructed, and practiced under God.
```

Article-ready sentence:

> Baxter помогает не противопоставлять благодать и порядок жизни. Если Бог действует благодатью, это не значит, что разум, наставление, порядок, средства и обязанности стали ненужными; это значит, что они должны быть под Богом, а не вместо Бога.

## 8. William Gurnall — prayer is weapon, not ornament

**Primary source:** William Gurnall, *The Christian in Complete Armour*.

Source anchors:

- CCEL prayer section PDF: https://www.ccel.org/ccel/gurnall/armour/files/gurnal05b.pdf
- CCEL collection: https://ccel.org/ccel/gurnall/armour/files/

Gurnall treats prayer as a necessary duty in spiritual warfare, used with other means. This matters because prayer is not merely religious atmosphere or inward mood; it is the believer’s God-appointed appeal for divine help.

Article-ready sentence:

> У Gurnall молитва — не религиозное украшение и не психологическая разгрузка, а труба войны: душа зовёт Бога на помощь, потому что враги падают не тогда, когда человек красиво говорит, а когда Бог восстаёт.

Pastoral use:

```text
Prayer as performance asks: “How did I sound?”
Prayer as technique asks: “Did I feel better?”
Prayer as faith asks: “Lord, arise; I cannot fight without You.”
```

## 9. Diagnostic map — four ways to misuse duties

### 9.1. Resting in duties

```text
Symptom: peace rises from performance itself.
Question: Would I still come to Christ if my best prayer needed cleansing?
Danger: duty becomes functional righteousness.
Remedy: cast duties at Christ’s feet.
```

### 9.2. Despising duties

```text
Symptom: grace-language becomes excuse for neglect.
Question: Do I claim Christ while refusing Christ’s appointed ways of feeding His people?
Danger: antinomian spiritual laziness.
Remedy: receive duties as means, not as saviors.
```

### 9.3. Formal duty

```text
Symptom: the act is present, the heart absent, and the absence is not mourned.
Question: Am I content that my body was in worship while my heart was elsewhere?
Danger: dead form.
Remedy: seek spiritual worship in spirit and truth.
```

### 9.4. Scrupulous/morbid duty

```text
Symptom: every duty becomes a verdict on salvation.
Question: Am I using prayer to come to Christ, or to decide whether I deserve to come?
Danger: the means of grace become instruments of self-torment.
Remedy: bring imperfect duties to a perfect Mediator.
```

## 10. Article-ready synthesis

> Обязанности — это плохой спаситель, но хорошие дороги. Формальное сердце проходит по дороге и гордится, что шло. Законническое сердце пытается купить дорогу своим шагом. Небрежное сердце говорит, что дороги не нужны. Живое сердце идёт по дороге ко Христу и знает: даже шаги нуждаются в крови Христа, но Христос всё равно зовёт идти.

Another compressed formula:

```text
Duties are not Christ.
Duties are not trash.
Duties are appointed means to bring needy souls to Christ.
The heart is tested not merely by whether it performs them,
but by whether it rests in them, despises them, or uses them to seek God.
```

Russian article formula:

> Проверка сердца не должна спрашивать только: “делаешь ли ты духовные обязанности?” Важнее спросить: “что твоё сердце делает с ними?” Опирается на них как на праведность? Презирает их как ненужные? Выполняет их как пустую форму? Или идёт через них к Богу, зная, что и сама молитва нуждается во Христе?
