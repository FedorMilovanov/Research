## 1. Почему V68 нужен после Boston / Owen / Charnock

V65–V67 закрепили главную карту:

```text
state of nature = sin reigns
state of grace = sin remains but does not reign
state of glory = sin no more
```

Но после этой карты появляется новая опасность: **как различить почти-христианина, ложную благодать и слабую настоящую благодать?**

V68 закрывает именно эту щель. Здесь нужны не десятки новых цитат, а несколько сильных пастырских предохранителей:

```text
Mead = almost Christian / convincing but not conversion
Shepard = false image of grace / common grace mistaken for true grace
Alleine = peace with sin / inordinate love of lawful comforts / conversion necessary
Guthrie = trial of saving interest + no forced one-pattern conversion story
Hooker = poor doubting soul must be drawn to Christ, not crushed by self-qualification
Brooks = true grace may exist without assurance
Marshall = sanctification begins from union with Christ, not self-repair
```

**Итоговый вывод:** четыре состояния не должны превратить серию в жестокую схему. Они должны дать точную диагностику: не утешать мёртвого как живого, но и не добивать живого как мёртвого.

## 2. Matthew Mead — almost Christian: не всякое религиозное движение есть благодать

### Primary locators

- Matthew Mead, `The Almost Christian Discovered`, Monergism PDF:  
  https://www.monergism.com/thethreshold/sdg/mead/The%20Almost%20Christian%20Discovered%20-%20Matthew%20Mead.pdf
- CCEL HTML locator:  
  https://www.ccel.org/ccel/mead_matthew/almost.html
- 1815 American edition note / transcription locator:  
  https://www.apuritansmind.com/wp-content/uploads/FREEEBOOKS/TheAlmostChristianDiscovered-MatthewMead.pdf

### Verified anchors

Mead begins from Acts 26:28: Agrippa was almost persuaded. Mead explicitly distinguishes:

```text
conscience touched ≠ heart renewed
convinced ≠ converted
profession broadly Christian ≠ true disciple of Christ
```

The table of contents and argument list show Mead's central diagnostic force: a man may have knowledge, gifts, profession, external duties, opposition to sin, hatred of some sin, vows, combat against sin, church membership, hopes of heaven, visible changes, zeal, prayer, suffering, external calling, Spirit operations, faith in some sense, love to God's people in some sense, obedience, a kind of sanctification, and still be but almost a Christian.

### Why this is gold

Mead prevents a naive diagnostic error:

```text
religious movement ≠ saving transition from nature to grace
conviction ≠ conversion
fear of consequences ≠ hatred of sin as sin
hatred of one sin ≠ hatred of all sin as against God
```

Especially strong is Mead's distinction between hating sin **for shame / self-damage / because one lust opposes another**, and hating sin because it wounds God, Christ, and the Spirit.

### Article-ready use

> Matthew Mead is a necessary antidote to shallow diagnostics. A person may be deeply religiously affected, externally reformed, fearful, zealous, and even opposed to many sins, and yet not be translated from nature into grace. The question is not only “is there movement?” but “what is the principle of the movement, and where does it end — in Christ or in self-preservation?”

### Guardrail

Do not use Mead to terrify every weak believer. Use him against false peace, not against bruised reeds. Pair him with Brooks / Hooker / Sibbes.

## 3. Thomas Shepard — false image of grace / common grace mistaken for true grace

### Primary locators

- Thomas Shepard, `The Sincere Convert`, Monergism PDF:  
  https://www.monergism.com/thethreshold/sdg/shepard/The%20Sincere%20Convert%20-%20Shepard.pdf
- Internet Archive 1650 edition locator:  
  https://archive.org/details/bim_early-english-books-1641-1700_the-sincere-convert-_shepard-thomas_1650_0
- Online Books / UPenn author locator for Shepard primary texts:  
  https://onlinebooks.library.upenn.edu/webbin/book/lookupname?key=Shepard%2C+Thomas%2C+1605-1649

### Verified anchors

Shepard gives a crucial category: the understanding can set up **a false image of grace**. The soul, ignorant of the height and excellency of true grace, may take common grace for true grace and then imagine itself to be in the estate of grace.

His anatomy is terrifyingly useful:

```text
fear of hell awakens the man
he wants peace and ease
he seeks “some grace” as medicine for fears
he reforms what troubles him
he reaches his own pitch
then he thinks he is a young beginner and rests there
```

Shepard says true grace is not merely fear-relief. It is Christ living, breathing, reigning, fighting and conquering in the soul.

### Why this is gold

This directly answers the user's concern: not every shifted priority, reform, fear, or religious seriousness should be called saving grace. But also not every weak beginning should be mocked. The key question is:

```text
Is grace used as medicine to silence fear,
or is Christ received as Lord, life, treasure, and reigning principle?
```

### Article-ready use

> Shepard exposes one of the deepest self-deceptions of the religious heart: a man may want enough “grace” to quiet his fears, but not Christ as the new life and reigning Lord of the soul. This is why pastoral diagnosis must ask not only what a person fears, but what he loves, what he receives, where he rests, and whether Christ himself becomes precious.

### Guardrail

Do not reduce Shepard to a harsh conversionist checklist. His point is not that true grace must be loud or dramatic, but that common grace and fear-relief must not be mistaken for union with Christ.

## 4. Joseph Alleine — conversion is necessary; peace with sin is deadly

### Primary locators

- Digital Puritan full text / IA transcription:  
  https://www.digitalpuritan.net/Digital%20Puritan%20Resources/Alleine%2C%20Joseph/An%20Alarm%20to%20the%20Unconverted%20%28IA%29.txt.html
- Internet Archive edition:  
  https://archive.org/details/alarmtounconvert00allerich
- NTS Library PDF locator:  
  https://www.ntslibrary.com/PDF%20Books/An%20Alarm%20to%20the%20Unconverted.pdf

### Verified anchors

Alleine's structure itself is useful:

```text
what conversion is not
what conversion is
necessity of conversion
marks of the unconverted
miseries of the unconverted
directions for conversion
motives to conversion
```

Important diagnostic anchors found:

```text
all powers and faculties corrupt in natural state unless purged from dead works
conscience cannot be truly pacified until soundly purified
peace with sin and peace in sin are worse than trouble
predominant love of the world is a sure evidence of an unsanctified heart
resting in a certain pitch of religion shows falling short of true grace
lawful comforts can keep a person from Christ as effectually as unlawful courses
where hatred/resistance/mortification is absent and evil habitually prevails, the person is in death
```

### Why this is gold

Alleine is helpful because he hits a subtle danger: not only scandalous sins, but **inordinate love of lawful comforts** can keep a person from Christ. This helps the article avoid a narrow “idols = only obviously evil things” approach.

### Article-ready use

> Alleine shows that a person may be kept from Christ not only by openly wicked courses, but also by the inordinate love of lawful comforts. Therefore idolatry is not measured only by whether an object is evil in itself; it is measured by what has the uppermost place in aims, love, estimation, and actual refuge.

### Guardrail

Use Alleine for unconverted security, not for crushing every legitimate comfort. The issue is not lawful comfort as such, but predominant love and functional refuge.

## 5. William Guthrie — trial of saving interest without forcing one conversion pattern

### Primary locators

- CCEL HTML:  
  https://www.ccel.org/g/guthrie/interest/int.htm
- CCEL Part I, `The Trial of a Saving Interest in Christ`:  
  https://www.ccel.org/g/guthrie/interest/int05.htm
- CCEL Chapter II, `Marks of a Saving Change`:  
  https://www.ccel.org/g/guthrie/interest/int07.htm
- CCEL Chapter IV, `Evidences of a Renewed State`:  
  https://www.ccel.org/g/guthrie/interest/int09.htm
- CCEL Part II, `How to Attain a Saving Interest in Christ`:  
  https://www.ccel.org/g/guthrie/interest/int10.htm
- CCEL Chapter II, `The Duty of Closing with God's Plan of Saving Sinners by Christ Jesus`:  
  https://www.ccel.org/g/guthrie/interest/int12.htm
- Monergism PDF:  
  https://www.monergism.com/thethreshold/sdg/williamguthrie/greatinterest_p.pdf

### Verified anchors

Guthrie is especially useful because he gives both self-examination and gospel direction.

He says the issue is whether a person has a true and special interest in Christ, and whether he may justly claim God's favour and salvation. He also explicitly notes two opposite cases:

```text
many pretend to Christ without ground
many who have ground remain in darkness without comfort
```

This is exactly the balance needed for the series.

Guthrie also refuses to force one conversion pattern:

```text
some called from the womb / early years
some called in a sovereign gospel-way
some called at the hour of death
more ordinary way: law-work and humiliation leading toward Christ
```

He warns that a preparatory law-work must not be turned into a universal negative test, because the Lord does not always observe the same plan with men.

On renewal, Guthrie's central mark is not a single emotional pattern but a **new creature**:

```text
new principle of life and motion
renewed understanding
renewed heart and affections
renewed outward members
renewed ways / worship / calling / interests
```

And when someone cannot see the marks, Guthrie does not send him into endless self-analysis only. He directs him to personally and heartily close with God's device of saving sinners by Christ Jesus.

### Why this is gold

Guthrie is one of the best sources for preventing two abuses:

```text
abuse 1: “If you cannot narrate conversion in this exact pattern, you are false.”
abuse 2: “If you have no marks, comfort yourself anyway.”
```

Guthrie gives a third way:

```text
examine by Scripture;
do not invent your own peace;
do not require one conversion template;
if marks are lacking, flee to Christ and close with the gospel offer.
```

### Article-ready use

> Guthrie gives a pastoral grammar better than panic or presumption. Some falsely claim Christ; others truly have Christ and yet lack comfort. Some conversions are gradual, some sudden, some from early life; therefore the question is not whether every story looks the same, but whether there is a saving interest in Christ and a real new creature. And if a man cannot find the marks, Guthrie does not tell him to manufacture assurance, but to close with Christ as God’s appointed way of saving sinners.

## 6. Thomas Hooker — the poor doubting Christian must be drawn to Christ

### Primary locators

- Digital Puritan PDF:  
  https://www.digitalpuritan.net/Digital%20Puritan%20Resources/Hooker%2C%20Thomas/%5BTH%5D%20The%20Poor%20Doubting%20Christian%20Drawn%20to%20Christ.pdf
- Internet Archive 1664 edition locator:  
  https://archive.org/details/bim_early-english-books-1641-1700_the-poor-doubting-christ_hooker-thomas_1664
- Monergism locator / eBook page:  
  https://www.monergism.com/poor-doubting-christian-drawn-christ-ebook

### Verified anchors

Hooker is not used here as the main four-state map, but as a pastor of doubting souls.

He identifies hindrances that keep men from coming to Christ:

```text
blind / careless / presumptuous security
attempt to save themselves by their own strength
reformation of life as self-amends
refusal to come because they lack enough enlargement, feeling, or qualification
```

The gospel answer is powerful:

```text
Christ requires no portion with us
He looks for poverty and emptiness
if thou hast nothing, he will have thee with thy nothing, provided thou wilt have him
best evidence: taking Christ as Saviour wholly and as husband only
```

### Why this is gold

Hooker guards tender souls from the opposite error of Mead/Shepard diagnostics. Mead warns against false peace; Hooker warns against false refusal to come to Christ because one does not feel qualified enough.

### Article-ready use

> Hooker is essential because the same doctrine that exposes false peace must not become a wall against Christ for a wounded sinner. The poor soul must not wait until it has enough inner enlargement or self-worthiness. Christ receives poverty and emptiness when the sinner receives Christ himself wholly and only.

### Guardrail

Do not use Hooker to comfort secure hypocrites. Use him for souls that are wounded, emptied, and hindered from coming to Christ by self-qualification or despair.

## 7. Thomas Brooks — true grace may exist without assurance

### Primary locators

- Thomas Brooks, `Heaven on Earth`, Monergism PDF:  
  https://www.monergism.com/thethreshold/sdg/brooks/Heaven%20on%20Earth%20-%20Thomas%20Brooks.pdf
- Digital Puritan PDF:  
  https://www.digitalpuritan.net/Digital%20Puritan%20Resources/Brooks%2C%20Thomas/Works%2C%20Vol.%20II/%5BTB%5D%20Heaven%20on%20Earth.pdf
- GraceGems HTML:  
  https://www.gracegems.org/Brooks/heaven_on_earth2.htm

### Verified anchors

Brooks is extremely important for protecting weak believers:

```text
man may have true grace and no assurance
God may deny assurance long and give it later
assurance may be lost
safe condition ≠ comfortable condition
little grace may yield little light
least true grace is sufficient to salvation
```

### Why this is gold

Brooks prevents a cruel diagnostic error:

```text
lack of assurance ≠ lack of grace
weak grace ≠ no grace
darkness of comfort ≠ state of nature
```

### Article-ready use

> Brooks separates state from sense. A soul may be safe in Christ and yet not enjoy the comfort of knowing it. Therefore a pastor must not diagnose by emotional brightness alone. Assurance is precious, but it is not identical with regeneration.

## 8. Walter Marshall — gospel sanctification, not self-repair

### Primary locators

- Walter Marshall, `The Gospel Mystery of Sanctification`, Monergism PDF:  
  https://www.monergism.com/thethreshold/articles/onsite/GospelMystery.pdf
- Digital Puritan PDF:  
  https://www.digitalpuritan.net/Digital%20Puritan%20Resources/Marshall%2C%20Walter/The%20Gospel%20Mystery%20of%20Sanctification%20Opened.pdf

### Verified anchors

Marshall is crucial for the sanctification side of the four-state map.

He says faith receives Christ himself with all necessary endowments for holy practice. Faith is the uniting grace by which the Spirit knits the mystical marriage between Christ and the believer. This is the grace by which we pass from the corrupt natural state to a new holy state in Christ, and from death in sin to life of righteousness.

He also warns against a doctrine that leaves wounded spirits trying first to get qualifications and then assurance, rather than applying the free promise of salvation by faith in Christ.

### Why this is gold

Marshall gives a strong answer to “discipline vs idolatry vs new birth”:

```text
holiness does not begin with self-repair
holy practice begins with receiving Christ by faith
sanctification flows from union with Christ
self-analysis without coming to Christ can deepen bondage
```

### Article-ready use

> Marshall protects the doctrine of change from becoming a sanctified self-help system. The corrupt natural state is not crossed by improved technique, but by union with Christ through faith; and the life of righteousness grows from Christ received, not from the sinner first manufacturing spiritual qualifications.

## 9. Diagnostic synthesis for the article

### 9.1. Four states plus pastoral sub-categories

```text
STATE OF NATURE
  - secure worldling
  - religious hypocrite
  - almost Christian
  - awakened but not converted
  - self-reforming legalist

STATE OF GRACE
  - weak believer
  - doubting believer
  - believer with little assurance
  - believer under remaining corruption
  - believer in slow mortification

STATE OF GLORY
  - no sin
  - no self-deception
  - no divided heart
  - no remaining corruption
```

### 9.2. What not to confuse

| Do not confuse | Why |
|---|---|
| Conviction with conversion | Mead / Shepard show conscience can be touched without heart renewed. |
| Fear of hell with hatred of sin | Mead distinguishes hating sin for shame or damage from hating sin as sin. |
| Reform with regeneration | Alleine / Hooker warn against self-saving reformation. |
| Lack of assurance with lack of grace | Brooks says true grace may exist without assurance. |
| One conversion pattern with all conversion | Guthrie refuses to force one preparatory-law-work pattern on all. |
| Lawful comfort with idol automatically | Alleine targets inordinate / predominant love, not lawful comfort as such. |
| Sanctification with self-repair | Marshall roots holiness in union with Christ. |

### 9.3. A compact article-ready diagnostic paragraph

> The Puritans do not let us flatten the heart into one category. Mead and Shepard warn that a man may be religiously awakened, outwardly reformed, fearful, zealous, and still only almost a Christian. Alleine warns that peace with sin, predominant love of the world, and resting in a chosen “pitch” of religion are deadly. But Guthrie, Hooker, Brooks, and Marshall prevent cruelty: not every true believer has a dramatic conversion story; not every weak saint has assurance; not every wounded soul must wait for more inward worthiness before coming to Christ; and holiness is not self-repair, but life flowing from union with Christ.

## 10. Practical pastoral map: what to say to whom

### To the secure unconverted

Use Alleine / Mead:

```text
Do not rest in profession, gifts, visible duties, external reform, or lawful comforts loved above Christ.
You need conversion, not religious varnish.
```

### To the almost Christian / awakened professor

Use Mead / Shepard:

```text
Do not confuse conviction, fear, gifts, vows, temporary reform, or common grace with saving union with Christ.
Ask whether Christ is received as life, Lord, treasure, and reigning principle.
```

### To the self-reforming legalist

Use Hooker / Marshall / Guthrie:

```text
Do not try to bring a portion to Christ.
Do not first manufacture qualifications.
Close with Christ as God's way of saving sinners.
```

### To the weak / doubting believer

Use Brooks / Hooker / Sibbes / Owen:

```text
Lack of assurance is not proof of no grace.
Little grace may give little light.
Sin remains but must not reign.
Come to Christ with poverty and emptiness.
```

### To the pastor / writer

Use the whole V68 block:

```text
Expose false peace without crushing weak grace.
Comfort weak grace without flattering false peace.
Preach conversion without requiring one conversion template.
Preach mortification without turning sanctification into self-help.
```

## 11. Best V68 “gold sentences” for the series

1. **Almost Christian is not a fifth state.** It is still inside the state of nature unless the person is savingly united to Christ.
2. **Weak believer is not almost Christian.** He may lack assurance, but if there is true grace, he is in the state of grace.
3. **Conviction is not conversion.** The conscience may be touched while the heart is not renewed.
4. **Reform is not regeneration.** A man can amend visible sins and still rest in himself.
5. **True grace may be small, dark, and trembling.** Its weakness does not make it false.
6. **False grace often seeks relief from fear more than Christ himself.**
7. **Lawful comfort becomes dangerous when it becomes predominant refuge.**
8. **Sanctification is not self-repair.** It flows from Christ received by faith.
9. **The article must hold both knives:** cut false peace and cut despair's knot.
10. **The final pastoral aim:** не утешать мёртвого как живого, но и не добивать живого как мёртвого.
