# V78 — Conscience, Scruples, Doubtful Things, Christian Liberty, and Humanly Bound Conscience

**Purpose:** add a Puritan/Reformed layer on **conscience**, **scrupulous conscience**, **doubtful things**, **things indifferent**, **Christian liberty**, **weak conscience**, **false accusation**, **seared conscience**, and **human commandments that bind where God has not bound**.

This layer is needed after V77 because temptation and consent are not the only subtle questions of the heart. A person may also be ruined by a conscience that:

```text
sleeps when it should accuse,
accuses when it should excuse,
doubts but refuses inquiry,
turns human preferences into divine law,
uses liberty as a cloak for lust,
or calls bondage “tenderness.”
```

Pastoral point:

```text
A tender conscience is not the same as a tyrannized conscience.
A free conscience is not the same as an unruled conscience.
A doubting conscience is not healed by pressure, but by truth.
A seared conscience is not healed by reassurance, but by awakening.
```

Article-ready sentence:

> Совесть может болеть не только от грязи греха, но и от ложной меры. Она может молчать там, где Бог обвиняет, и обвинять там, где Бог оправдывает. Поэтому зрелая пастырская диагностика не просто “давит на совесть”, а учит совесть заново стоять перед Богом, Его Словом и Евангелием.

---

## 1. Why this layer matters for the heart-series

Earlier volumes have built the map:

```text
four states,
false peace,
temporary faith,
weak grace,
melancholy,
backsliding,
holy affections,
worldliness,
bosom sin,
temptation and consent.
```

V78 adds the missing surgical layer: **what happens when the internal court of conscience is miscalibrated?**

Without this layer, the article may fall into two errors:

1. **Legalistic over-binding:** treating every personal fear, custom, preference, or pastoral application as a divine law.
2. **Antinomian under-binding:** treating freedom from human commandments as freedom from holiness, obedience, and love.

Puritan casuistry is more careful:

```text
God alone binds the conscience absolutely.
The Word must rule conscience.
Human authority has real but subordinate authority.
Things indifferent may become good or bad by end, use, love, scandal, command, or circumstance.
Doubt requires inquiry, not proud action.
Tenderness must be distinguished from scrupulosity.
Liberty must be distinguished from license.
```

## 2. Confessional anchor — 1689 / Savoy / Westminster: liberty is both freedom from bondage and freedom for obedience

### 2.1. 1689 London Baptist Confession, Chapter 21

Primary anchor:

- https://www.the1689confession.com/1689/chapter-21
- https://founders.org/library/chapter-21-christian-liberty-and-liberty-of-conscience/

The 1689 Confession is the crucial Baptist anchor for this layer. Chapter 21 says Christian liberty consists in freedom from:

```text
guilt of sin,
condemning wrath of God,
severity and curse of the law,
this present evil world,
bondage to Satan,
dominion of sin,
evil of afflictions,
fear and sting of death,
victory of the grave,
everlasting damnation,
```

and also in:

```text
free access to God,
obedience not from slavish fear,
but from childlike love and willing mind.
```

Paragraph 2 gives the key Protestant/Baptist line:

```text
God alone is Lord of the conscience.
```

Therefore doctrines and commandments contrary to the Word, or not contained in it, cannot be imposed as conscience-binding divine law.

Paragraph 3 guards the opposite error:

```text
Christian liberty must not become a pretext for practicing sin or cherishing sinful lust.
```

Article-ready sentence:

> Баптистская реформатская исповедальная линия не говорит: “совесть свободна — значит, делай что хочешь”. Она говорит сильнее и точнее: совесть свободна от человеческого господства, чтобы служить Богу без рабского страха, из сыновней любви и добровольного послушания.

### 2.2. Savoy Declaration and Westminster parallel

Primary anchors:

- Savoy Declaration: https://creedsandconfessions.org/savoy-declaration.html
- Westminster chapter 20: https://www.apuritansmind.com/westminster-standards/chapter-20/

Savoy 21 closely parallels this language: liberty from guilt, wrath, curse, world, Satan, dominion of sin, fear of death, and liberty of access to God; God alone is Lord of conscience; Christian liberty is not a cloak for lust.

This means the Baptist use of chapter 21 is not isolated. It stands inside a broader Reformed confessional grammar.

Useful framing:

```text
Westminster / Savoy / 1689 together protect the same two edges:
- no man may bind where God has not bound;
- no sinner may call lust “liberty.”
```

## 3. William Ames — conscience can err, doubt, accuse falsely, or become seared

Primary anchors:

- EEBO item: https://quod.lib.umich.edu/e/eebo/A69129.0001.001?view=toc
- TCP text: https://ota.bodleian.ox.ac.uk/repository/xmlui/bitstream/handle/20.500.12024/A69129/A69129.html?isAllowed=y&sequence=5
- Internet Archive scan: https://archive.org/details/conscpo00ames

Ames is a major source because he gives not just exhortation, but taxonomy.

### 3.1. Conscience is not a mystical feeling; it is practical judgment before God

Ames treats conscience as a practical judgment. It accuses, excuses, troubles, comforts, and reasons. This matters because a conscience must be **instructed**, not merely obeyed blindly in every confused motion.

Diagnostic point:

```text
Conscience is not sovereign.
Conscience is a witness and judge under God.
Therefore a false conscience must be corrected, not enthroned.
```

Article-ready sentence:

> Совесть не является маленьким богом внутри человека. Она должна быть не заглушена и не обожествлена, а приведена под суд Божьего Слова.

### 3.2. Erroneous conscience: one may sin either by following it or by violating it

Ames distinguishes an erroneous conscience from a doubting/scrupulous conscience. His subtle point: when conscience errs positively, one may be entangled so that sin appears on either side — either in the matter of the action or in acting against what one believes to be duty.

Diagnostic point:

```text
The solution is not “just obey your conscience.”
The solution is: correct the conscience by the rule of God.
```

Article-ready sentence:

> “Поступай по совести” — хорошая фраза только тогда, когда совесть учится у Бога. Если совесть ошибается, она может ранить человека даже тогда, когда он формально “слушается совести”.

### 3.3. Doubting conscience: do not act proudly where the mind remains practically unresolved

Ames says practical doubts require diligent inquiry so that truth may be clearly perceived. He also says one must not act against one’s own judgment merely out of respect to other men’s authority.

This is crucial for pastoral care:

```text
A doubting conscience is not healed by pressure.
It is healed by patient inquiry under Scripture.
```

Article-ready sentence:

> Сомневающуюся совесть нельзя лечить пастырским нажимом: “делай, потому что я сказал”. Её надо вести к ясности перед Богом, иначе человек привыкает не к послушанию, а к внутреннему насилию.

### 3.4. False accusation: conscience can condemn where it should excuse

Ames explicitly notes that an evil conscience may falsely accuse and condemn where it ought to excuse. This is the missing nuance in many heart-diagnostics.

Diagnostic point:

```text
Not every accusation of conscience is the voice of the Spirit.
Some accusations must be examined, denied, and healed.
```

Article-ready sentence:

> Нельзя считать всякое внутреннее обвинение голосом Святого Духа. Совесть может обвинять справедливо, а может клеветать на благодать, особенно у тревожной, омрачённой или искушаемой души.

### 3.5. Hard / stupid / seared conscience vs tender conscience

Ames distinguishes degrees of evil conscience: benumbed, stupid, seared, troubled, desperate. He also contrasts hard conscience with tender conscience — a tender conscience is moved by the Word of God.

Important nuance:

```text
A tender conscience is responsive to God’s Word.
A scrupulous conscience is often enslaved by fear, inference, custom, or human pressure.
```

Article-ready sentence:

> Нежная совесть быстро отзывается на Божье Слово. Скрупулёзная совесть часто быстро отзывается на страх. Это не одно и то же.

### 3.6. Troubled conscience in unbeliever vs troubled conscience in believer

Ames gives a brilliant distinction:

```text
In the unbeliever, a justly troubled conscience may be a preparation toward repentance and faith.
But the remedy is not mere comfort; the person must be brought into another state by conversion and faith.

In a believer, after grievous sin, conscience may accuse honestly and require true repentance.
But in believers, many accusations must be examined because the major or minor in the conscience’s syllogism may be false.
```

Article-ready sentence:

> Одно и то же чувство ужаса может требовать разного лечения. Невозрождённого нельзя утешать так, будто он уже во Христе; верующего нельзя оставлять в отчаянии так, будто тяжёлое обвинение совести автоматически отменяет Христа.

## 4. William Perkins — cases of conscience as pastoral medicine, not curiosity

Primary anchors:

- EEBO item: https://quod.lib.umich.edu/e/eebo/A09365.0001.001?view=toc
- Online Books page: https://onlinebooks.library.upenn.edu/webbin/book/lookupid?key=uma09365
- Internet Archive scan: https://archive.org/details/bim_early-english-books-1475-1640_the-whole-treatise-of-th_perkins-william_1606

Perkins’ *Whole Treatise of the Cases of Conscience* is important because it shows that Puritan heart-diagnosis was not a crude checklist. It was a pastoral discipline aimed at bringing souls to God, resolving fears, guiding obedience, and healing misjudgment.

The item’s title and table show a work explicitly organized around “cases of conscience,” with questions propounded and resolved. This makes it useful as an early Puritan anchor for the whole research pack.

Article-ready sentence:

> У Перкинса “случаи совести” — это не любопытная моральная казуистика, а пастырская медицина: как привести человека к Божьему благоволению, как отличить страх от веры, как направить совесть к покаянию, Христу и новому послушанию.

## 5. Richard Baxter — human laws, pastoral authority, and conscience: real authority, but not divine absolutism

Primary anchor:

- EEBO item: https://quod.lib.umich.edu/e/eebo/A26892.0001.001?view=toc
- Section on whether human laws bind conscience: https://quod.lib.umich.edu/e/eebo/A26892.0001.001/1:8.5.1?rgn=div3;view=fulltext

Baxter is gold because he refuses simplistic answers.

He distinguishes:

```text
conscience in the broad sense,
conscience in the strict theological sense,
human obligation,
derivatively divine obligation,
and direct divine obligation.
```

He says it is proper to say that we are bound by God where God has authorized rulers to command. Yet he also says man is not God, and human laws are subordinate to God’s laws in nature and Scripture. A human law does not oblige to sin or against the common good.

This helps the article avoid two modern distortions:

1. **Anti-authority individualism:** “No man may bind my conscience; therefore no human authority matters.”
2. **Authoritarian spiritual abuse:** “Authority said it; therefore God Himself binds you absolutely.”

Article-ready sentence:

> Бакстер не растворяет совесть в индивидуализме и не отдаёт её людям. Человеческая власть реальна, но производна; Божья власть абсолютна. Поэтому совесть не должна презирать порядок, но и не должна принимать человеческое распоряжение за голос Бога там, где Бог этого не сказал.

## 6. Samuel Bolton — Christian freedom is neither legalism nor antinomianism

Primary anchors:

- Monergism page: https://www.monergism.com/true-bounds-christian-freedom-ebook
- Monergism PDF: https://www.monergism.com/thethreshold/sdg/bolton/The%20True%20Bounds%20of%20Christian%20Fr%20-%20Samuel%20Bolton.pdf
- Digital Puritan: https://digitalpuritan.net/samuel-bolton/

Bolton’s *True Bounds of Christian Freedom* is the perfect complement to Chapter 21 of the confessions.

He protects both edges:

```text
We are free from the law as covenant of condemnation.
We are not free from the moral law as rule of grateful obedience.
```

The article should use Bolton to prevent confusion between:

```text
freedom from justification-by-law,
and freedom from obedience.
```

Article-ready sentence:

> Евангелие освобождает не от святости, а от рабского способа искать жизнь через закон. Свобода во Христе не уничтожает послушание; она меняет его корень — от страха осуждения к сыновней любви.

## 7. Goodwin / Owen — distressed conscience and practical cases

Primary anchors:

- Goodwin, *Certain Select Cases Resolved*: https://www.digitalpuritan.net/Digital%20Puritan%20Resources/Goodwin%2C%20Thomas/Works%20%28Vol.3%29%20Ind%20Titles/%5BTG%5D%20Certain%20Select%20Cases%20Resolved.pdf
- Owen, *Several Practical Cases of Conscience Resolved*: https://www.monergism.com/several-practical-cases-conscience-resolved-ebook

Goodwin’s title explicitly aims at comforting distressed consciences. Owen’s practical cases belong in the same casuistic tradition: conscience, repentance, assurance, obedience, and difficult spiritual questions.

Use them not as a main axis for V78, but as supporting sources for the pastoral point:

```text
Conscience must be resolved, not merely intensified.
Some souls need awakening.
Some need instruction.
Some need correction.
Some need comfort.
Some need to stop defending sin.
Some need to stop accusing Christ’s grace.
```

Article-ready sentence:

> Настоящая пастырская работа с совестью не всегда усиливает боль. Иногда она должна усилить боль, иногда очистить боль, иногда объяснить боль, а иногда запретить боли говорить от имени Бога.

## 8. Diagnostic grid — how to tell what kind of conscience is speaking

### 8.1. Hard or seared conscience

Signs:

```text
little movement under ordinary sin,
only awakened by severe judgments,
rejoicing in sin,
refusal of reform,
giving oneself to known sin.
```

Pastoral response:

```text
Do not soothe.
Awaken by law, judgment, holiness, and the danger of hardening.
Bring to Christ, but not with cheap peace.
```

### 8.2. Tender conscience

Signs:

```text
quickly moved by the Word,
sensitive to known sin,
willing to be corrected,
not defensive when Scripture speaks,
seeking holiness from God.
```

Pastoral response:

```text
Encourage.
Instruct.
Do not crush.
Help it distinguish God’s Word from fear and human additions.
```

### 8.3. Scrupulous conscience

Signs:

```text
over-accuses,
cannot distinguish command from inference,
treats possibility as guilt,
confuses fear with holiness,
can be manipulated by strong personalities.
```

Pastoral response:

```text
Do not merely say “stop worrying.”
Clarify Scripture.
Separate God’s command from human rule.
Teach Christian liberty slowly.
```

### 8.4. Doubting conscience in practical matters

Signs:

```text
uncertain whether an act is lawful,
not yet resolved by Scripture, wisdom, circumstance, and counsel,
pressured to act before clarity.
```

Pastoral response:

```text
Do not push it by authority alone.
Require inquiry.
Do not let it use doubt as permanent evasion.
```

### 8.5. False accusing conscience in a believer

Signs:

```text
condemns where Christ does not condemn,
turns weakness into proof of reprobation,
turns intrusive temptation into beloved sin,
turns melancholy into divine sentence,
refuses gospel reasoning.
```

Pastoral response:

```text
Examine the conscience’s syllogism.
Deny false premises.
Apply justification, adoption, Christ’s intercession, and promises.
```

### 8.6. Licentious conscience

Signs:

```text
uses liberty to cherish lust,
rejects duty as legalism,
confuses freedom from condemnation with freedom from obedience,
resents the moral law as a guide.
```

Pastoral response:

```text
Use 1689/Savoy/WCF chapter 21 and Bolton.
Christian liberty exists for holy service, not self-rule.
```

### 8.7. Humanly bound conscience

Signs:

```text
treats man-made rules as divine law,
requires implicit faith and blind obedience,
confuses pastoral application with Scripture itself,
condemns others where God has left liberty.
```

Pastoral response:

```text
Teach “God alone is Lord of conscience.”
Distinguish Scripture from tradition, prudence, counsel, church order, and personal conviction.
```

## 9. Article-ready “gold” formulations

### 9.1. On tenderness vs scrupulosity

> Нежная совесть боится нарушить Божье Слово. Скрупулёзная совесть боится всего, что похоже на опасность. Первая нуждается в укреплении; вторая — в освобождении под властью Писания.

### 9.2. On liberty vs license

> Христианская свобода не говорит сердцу: “теперь ты сам себе господин”. Она говорит: “человек больше не господин твоей совести, грех больше не господин твоей воли, закон больше не проклинает тебя во Христе — теперь служи Богу как сын, а не как раб”.

### 9.3. On human authority

> Пастор может учить, убеждать, применять, советовать, предупреждать и дисциплинировать по Слову. Но пастор не может превратить свою мудрую меру в Божью заповедь там, где Бог её не дал.

### 9.4. On over-accusation

> Не всякое обвинение совести надо немедленно принимать как Божий приговор. Иногда совесть — свидетель истины; иногда она повторяет страх, привычку, меланхолию, человеческое давление или ложный вывод.

### 9.5. On under-accusation

> Но и молчание совести не доказывает чистоты. Совесть может быть не только раненой, но и оглушённой, усыплённой, ожесточённой и приученной не чувствовать грех.

### 9.6. On doubtful actions

> Сомнение не всегда запрещает навсегда, но оно запрещает гордую поспешность. Там, где совесть не видит дороги, её надо не тащить, а просвещать.

### 9.7. On things indifferent

> “Безразличное” не значит “духовно пустое”. Вещь может быть безразличной по природе, но стать доброй или дурной по цели, обстоятельству, любви, соблазну, употреблению и отношению сердца к Богу.

## 10. Compact thesis for the series

```text
The heart must not be ruled by humanly manufactured guilt.
The heart must not be excused by self-manufactured liberty.
Conscience must be freed from men, awakened from sin, instructed by Scripture, softened by grace, corrected when false, and comforted by Christ.
```

Russian thesis:

> Сердце нельзя лечить ни человеческой тиранией, ни самовольной свободой. Совесть должна быть свободна от человеческого господства, пробуждена от греха, наставлена Писанием, смягчена благодатью, исправлена там, где она лжёт, и утешена Христом там, где она справедливо или ошибочно ранена.
