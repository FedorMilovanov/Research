## 0. Главная правка

Теперь задача не расширять список имён, а **чистить и ранжировать кейсы по проверяемости**.

> **Кейс можно включать в статью только если он держится на источнике достаточного уровня для той силы утверждения, которую мы делаем.**

Нельзя:

- строить кейс на слухах;
- брать пересказы без первичного документа;
- писать “он сделал X”, если есть только allegation;
- писать “доказано”, если есть только credible report;
- смягчать судебно признанный факт языком “moral failure”;
- делать человека “Диотрефом”, если источник показывает другой тип проблемы.

## 1. Лестница источников

### Уровень A — можно уверенно использовать как факт

- судебный приговор;
- guilty plea;
- официальное заявление суда / прокуратуры;
- независимый investigative report;
- официальное церковное решение / statement elders / presbytery / board;
- первичный документ организации;
- собственное публичное признание, если оно подтверждает факт.

### Уровень B — можно использовать осторожно

- крупные журналистские расследования;
- Christianity Today / AP / Washington Post / NYT / Houston Chronicle / Religion News Service;
- признанные христианские издания с редакторской ответственностью;
- документы, опубликованные через DocumentCloud;
- несколько независимых источников, совпадающих по фактам.

### Уровень C — только как указатель, не как основание

- энциклопедические пересказы;
- блоги;
- Twitter/X threads;
- подкасты;
- пересказы без ссылок;
- сайты с явной полемической целью.

### Уровень D — не использовать

- слухи;
- “все знают”;
- личные впечатления;
- неподтверждённые обвинения;
- анонимные заявления без проверки;
- имена, которые вспоминают в дискуссиях, но без документов.

> **Сила утверждения не должна быть сильнее силы источника.**

## 2. Четыре формы утверждений

### 2.1. Судебный факт

Можно писать:

> “X признал вину / был осуждён / получил приговор по делу…”

### 2.2. Независимое расследование / credible finding

Можно писать:

> “Независимый отчёт счёл обвинения credible / substantiated…”

Нельзя писать:

> “суд доказал”, если суда не было.

### 2.3. Официальное церковное решение

Можно писать:

> “Совет / пресвитеры / церковная сеть сняли / признали поведение disqualifying / отправили в дисциплину…”

### 2.4. Allegation / disputed case

Можно писать только:

> “X был обвинён; X denied; report/organization found Y credible / did not find / case unresolved…”

## 4. Новый протокол включения кейса в статью

Для каждого кейса перед публикацией:

1. **Назвать категорию**, а не просто имя: ложное учение, непригодный характер, тяжкое преступление, системное покрытие, ложное восстановление, служитель типа Диотрефа, Корей в общине.
2. **Назвать уровень источника**: A/B/C/D.
3. **Сформулировать только то, что источник позволяет**: guilty plea, credible finding, church discipline, allegation, denied allegation, unresolved.
4. **Не смягчать и не усиливать**: преступление не “moral failure”; allegation не “доказанный факт”; church discipline не “судебный приговор”.
5. **Сказать, чему кейс учит**, а не просто выставить человека.

## UPDATE v2026-06-25K — PURITAN GOLD: HEART-KEEPING, COUNTERFEIT SINCERITY, AND TENDERNESS WITHOUT NAIVETY

### Purpose
This layer deepens the source-first framework for false repentance without adding new files. It adds classic Puritan diagnostics that help distinguish three things often confused in church crises:

1. a bruised reed that must not be crushed;
2. a hypocritical confession that must not be trusted too quickly;
3. a disciplined person who may be received as a brother yet not restored to office.

### William Perkins — case-divinity and exact conscience work
Perkins belongs in the background of the whole series because he represents Puritan case-divinity: theology applied to conscience, evidence, self-deception, assurance, and practical spiritual judgment. His usefulness here is methodological: do not treat repentance as a vague mood. Ask concrete case questions:

- What exactly was confessed?
- Was the confession voluntary or forced by exposure?
- What was concealed until evidence made concealment impossible?
- Did the person seek God, or only relief from shame?
- What fruit appears when the same temptation, access, status, and opportunity return?

Source direction: William Perkins, *A Case of Conscience*; *Of the Nature and Practice of Repentance*; later collected Works. Use modern critical editions where available; otherwise use early modern scans only with careful normalization.

### Richard Sibbes — tenderness toward true bruising, not softness toward manipulation
Sibbes is crucial for balance. A church that only exposes can crush the weak; a church that only comforts can protect the manipulator. The bruised reed principle must be applied to the truly bruised conscience, not to a leader using “brokenness” as reputation management.

Diagnostic use:

- True bruising makes a person more tender toward Christ and toward those harmed.
- False bruising makes the person central: “look how broken I am,” while victims and facts become secondary.
- Pastoral tenderness must not become a shield against truth.

Series formula: do not crush the bruised reed; do not perfume the unclean wound.

Source direction: Richard Sibbes, *The Bruised Reed and Smoking Flax*.

### John Flavel — keeping the heart: the root test beneath visible behaviour
Flavel’s “heart-keeping” is a major source for the series because it shifts attention from behaviour management to the spring of behaviour. A pastor may stop the visible scandal because he lost access, fear, status, money, or secrecy. That is not yet mortification. The test is whether the heart-source is being watched before God.

Diagnostic use:

- Did the person lose opportunity, or did he begin keeping the heart?
- Is the old root still protected by new vocabulary?
- Does the person now fear the first motions of sin, or only the public consequences?
- Is there new watchfulness in money, speech, sexuality, anger, secrecy, and authority?

Series formula: stopping the leak is not the same as purifying the spring.

Source direction: John Flavel, *Keeping the Heart* / *The Touchstone of Sincerity*. Public-domain access point: Internet Archive, “On Keeping the Heart” by John Flavel.

### Thomas Manton — fruit worthy of repentance, not religious language alone
Manton’s usefulness is in ordinary, careful, text-driven moral examination. He should be used to strengthen the line that repentance must be visible in fruits suitable to its claim. Where the scandal was public, the fruit cannot be merely private; where the sin damaged trust, the fruit must include patient non-demand of trust.

Diagnostic use:

- Is the fruit proportionate to the claim?
- Does the person show submission to correction?
- Does he accept that time is part of testing?
- Does he cease demanding the same influence that made the sin more destructive?

Series formula: repentance speaks; fruit translates.

Source direction: Thomas Manton, sermons on Matthew 3, James, Hebrews, and Psalm 119 in collected Works.

### Thomas Boston — external reformation can coexist with an unchanged nature
Boston helps guard against naive restoration. External reformation can happen through pressure, fear, or prudence. That does not automatically show renewed nature or pastoral fitness. In church crises, this matters because visible calm after scandal may simply mean access has been removed or public danger has passed.

Diagnostic use:

- Is the change evangelical or merely circumstantial?
- Has the heart turned Godward, or has the environment changed?
- Is there new love of holiness, or just fear of another exposure?
- Does the person grieve as one who sinned against God, or as one whose future was interrupted?

Series formula: a caged wolf is not a converted shepherd.

Source direction: Thomas Boston, *Human Nature in Its Fourfold State*; *The Crook in the Lot*; Marrow-context works for gospel/free grace balance.

### Thomas Brooks — Satan minimizes sin by renaming it
Brooks remains one of the most useful sources for language analysis. The pastoral scandal vocabulary often works exactly like a device: make sin seem small, ordinary, understandable, universal, private, or already resolved.

Modern phrases to classify under this pattern:

- “a difficult season” instead of a pattern of sin;
- “mistakes were made” instead of “I sinned”;
- “if anyone was hurt” instead of “I harmed specific people”;
- “boundaries were unclear” instead of “I crossed boundaries”;
- “we all need grace” instead of “I must accept discipline.”

Series formula: the first refuge of false repentance is often not denial, but vocabulary.

Source direction: Thomas Brooks, *Precious Remedies Against Satan’s Devices*.

### Integrated diagnostic: the K-layer test
When a leader says “I repent,” ask:

1. Sibbes test — is this a truly bruised conscience, or a performance of brokenness?
2. Flavel test — is the heart being kept, or only the scandal managed?
3. Manton test — are there fruits proportionate to the claim?
4. Boston test — is the change evangelical or merely circumstantial?
5. Brooks test — has sin been named truthfully, or linguistically reduced?
6. Perkins test — does the case withstand exact conscience-level questioning?

### Gold sentence for future article
False repentance asks the church to believe a new story about the same heart. True repentance lets the church examine the heart-source, the fruit, the vocabulary, the restitution, the time, and the willingness to lose office without losing Christ.

## Core theological principle extracted

A powerful case is not the case with the most dramatic story, but the case where the article can say less and prove more.

The series should teach readers to distinguish:

- sin confessed vs sin exposed;
- apology vs repentance;
- forgiveness vs office;
- ministry fruit in hearers vs qualification of the minister;
- public usefulness vs biblical fitness;
- source lead vs printable proof.
